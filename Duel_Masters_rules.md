## 0. Introduction

**000. Purpose of this Document**
**000.1.** This document compiles the detailed rules of Duel Masters. It is not necessary for all players to be completely familiar with it, but it is useful when you want to know more specific details or resolve questions that arise during an actual game.

## 1. Basics of the Game

**100. Things Needed for the Game**
**100.1.** Duel Masters is a competitive game for two players.
**100.2.** Normally, decks are constructed according to the Hall of Fame regulation rules. A deck must consist of exactly 40 cards, no more and no less.
* **100.2a** Except for cards with text explicitly stating otherwise, a deck can contain up to 4 copies of a card with the same name.
**100.3.** Separately from the deck, up to 8 additional cards can be used for the Hyperspatial Zone.
* **100.3a** Except for cards with text explicitly stating otherwise, the Hyperspatial Zone can contain up to 4 copies of a card with the same name.
**100.4.** You may choose whether to use an Ultra GR (Gacha Range) Zone. If you choose to use it, you can add exactly 12 cards separately from your deck for the Ultra GR Zone.
* **100.4a** Except for cards with text explicitly stating otherwise, the Ultra GR Zone can contain up to 2 copies of a card with the same name.
**100.5** At the start of the game, only 1 set of cards can exist in the Battle Zone.
*Examples of cards that can exist in the Battle Zone at the start of the game:*
* 《Forbidden ~Sealed X~》
* 《FORBIDDEN STAR ~The Last Day of the World~》
* 《Origin of Destruction, Zerom》
* 《Forbidden New Year》

**101. Absolute Rules of Duel Masters**
**101.1. Card Effects Beat the Rules**
When a card's text directly contradicts the rules, the card's text takes precedence. (The card only ignores the rule applied to that specific situation.)
**101.2. "Cannot" Effects Beat "Can" Effects**
When an effect stating you "can" do something and an effect stating you "cannot" do something for a certain event exist simultaneously, the "cannot" effect takes precedence. Furthermore, if there is an effect that "ignores" a "cannot" effect, the "cannot" effect is ignored and loses its power. However, since cards beat the rules, a "can" effect on a card beats a "cannot" rule. (For example, a creature with "Speed Attacker" beats the summoning sickness rule and can attack.)
**101.3. Do Everything You Can**
Even if a part of a card's instructions cannot be performed, do everything that can be performed, and ignore the part that cannot.
* **101.3a** If an effect has a condition for its activation such as "When you do...", it triggers when the action serving as the condition is attempted, regardless of the ultimate outcome of that action.
**101.4. Effect Processing Prioritizes S-Triggers and Always Starts with the Turn Player's Effects**
When multiple effects exist simultaneously, S-Trigger effects are always processed first. Also, when effects from the turn player and the non-turn player exist simultaneously, the turn player's effects are processed first. Only after the processing of one effect is completely finished do you move on to processing the next effect.
* **101.4a** When multiple effects occur simultaneously, first process the S-Trigger effects in the order of Turn Player, then Non-Turn Player. After that, from the remaining effects, the Turn Player resolves the effects their cards have in any order they choose. If another effect is generated during the processing of one effect, it enters a standby state, and the currently resolving effect is prioritized. Then, remaining effects are processed in the chosen order. Once all Turn Player effects are completely processed, the Non-Turn Player's effects are processed.
* **101.4b** S-Back, G-Strike, and Sabaki Z are processed at the same timing as S-Triggers. If multiple of these effects occur simultaneously, they are processed in the order of Turn Player, then Non-Turn Player.
* **101.4c** If a Turn Player's effect is generated during the process of resolving a Non-Turn Player's effect, the Turn Player's effect takes priority and is processed after the currently resolving effect finishes.
* **101.4d** Other effects cannot interrupt the processing of an effect, except for Replacement Effects. However, if an effect's instructions dictate using the effect of another spell, etc., it is resolved as part of that processing.
* **101.4e** Battles generated by an ability or a spell's effect are considered part of that ability or spell's effect until the end of that battle.
* **101.4f** Abilities that trigger "when battling" take effect immediately, even during a battle generated by an ability or spell effect.

**101.5. Replacement Effects Apply Only Once Per Event**
Only one Replacement Effect can be applied to a single event. An "event" refers to any occurrence that can happen during a game. If an event has already been replaced, it cannot be replaced again.
* **101.5a** When a Replacement Effect modifies a part of a sentence separated by a punctuation mark, the text within that bounded section is not replaced any further. Even within the same ability, text separated by a different punctuation mark (like "If you do so,") is considered a different event and is not treated as replaced. The original effect is processed as a single effect, including the replaced portion.
* **101.5b** When multiple Replacement Effects exist simultaneously, they are applied prioritizing the Turn Player's Replacement Effects, similar to standard effect processing. If the same player has multiple Replacement Effects, they can choose which one to apply.
**102. Players**
**102.1.** A player is a person participating in the game. The "turn player" is the player whose turn is currently in progress, and the other player is called the "non-turn player."
**102.2.** The opponent is the other player besides yourself.

**103. How to Start the Game**
**103.1.** Before starting the game, each player prepares their deck and the cards used in the game. At this time, double-sided cards may be set aside.
**103.2.** After that, hand the deck to the opponent to confirm it is exactly 40 cards, and have them shuffle or cut it. A player's deck is thereafter referred to as the deck.
**103.3.** Next, move to the game start procedure. At this time, if there are any instructions to do something at the start of the game, follow them. After that, each player places the top 5 cards of their deck face down as shields, and draws 5 cards to form their hand.
**103.4.** Next, decide who goes first. Play Rock-Paper-Scissors, and the winner goes first. (You cannot choose to go second.)
**103.5.** The player going first starts their turn. (The player going first does not draw a card on their first turn.)

**104. Winning Conditions**
**104.1.** The game ends immediately if either player wins or loses.
**104.2.** The conditions for winning the game are as follows:
* **104.2a** When you attack the opponent with a creature and the target of the attack is not changed, if the opponent has 0 shields, you immediately win the game.
* **104.2b** When the opponent's deck reaches 0 cards, you immediately win the game. (If there are 0 cards in the deck even for a split second during the processing of an effect, it is considered that the deck has reached 0 cards.)
* **104.2c** You may win or lose by card effects. If you meet both a win condition and a lose condition simultaneously through these effects, you win.

**105. Orientation / Status**
**105.1.** Duel Masters cards have an orientation. The vertical state of the card is called the untapped state, and the horizontal state is called the tapped state.
**105.2.** The untapped state indicates that a card is available for use. Normally, all tapped cards become untapped at the beginning of the turn.
**105.3.** The tapped state indicates that a card has been used. You tap a card when a creature attacks or when you pay costs from the mana zone.
**105.4.** You cannot change the orientation of a card arbitrarily.
* **105.4a** You can only tap creatures and Tamaseeds in the Battle Zone, and cards in the Mana Zone. You cannot change the orientation of cards in other zones.
**105.5.** For Fields, there is an orientation called "upside down." Cards in this state are not returned to the upright position by untap effects.
**105.6.** You cannot tap a card that is already tapped, or untap a card that is already untapped. (It is possible to choose a tapped creature with an ability or effect that says "tap," but it does not count as having changed its state.)

**106. Civilizations**
**106.1.** There are five civilizations in Duel Masters: "Light", "Water", "Darkness", "Fire", and "Nature".
**106.2.** Normally, a card has one or more of the five civilizations listed above. A card's civilization is referenced by the color of its mana symbol, the background color of its card type, its frame color, etc.
* **106.2a** A mono-civilization card has exactly one of the five civilizations.
* **106.2b** A multi-civilization card has two or more of the five civilizations, and is called a multi-colored card.
* **106.2c** A card without a civilization is called a colorless card.
* **106.2d** Each civilization has a designated color. Light is yellow, Water is blue, Darkness is black (or purple), Fire is red, and Nature is green. Colorless cards are represented by white.
**106.3.** When instructed to choose a civilization by a card, you must choose one of the five civilizations above. You cannot choose "colorless."
**106.4.** A civilization may be added to a card by an effect.

**107. Mana**
**107.1.** Mana is the fundamental resource in Duel Masters. Players use mana to pay the costs of summoning creatures and casting spells.
**107.2.** Mana is generated by tapping a card placed in the mana zone, equal to the numerical value written on its mana symbol (usually 1). The generated mana has one of the civilizations of the tapped card.

**108. Numbers**
**108.1.** In the game of Duel Masters, basically only integers are used.
* **108.1a** When choosing a number for an effect, you cannot choose a negative number.
* **108.1b** A creature's power can become negative due to minus modifications. However, when referencing that creature's power, it is treated as 0. For example, even if you choose a creature in the Battle Zone whose power has been reduced by 6000 with 《Hell Scrapper》, you cannot choose other creatures such that their total power equals 11000.
* **108.1c** In Duel Masters, ∞ (infinity) is treated as a number larger than any number you can choose. If a creature with ∞ power receives a power modification, its power remains ∞. However, if its power receives a -∞ modification, it is destroyed.
**108.2.** When calculations involving numbers are performed in a Duel Masters game, they are processed in the following order: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

**109. Cards**
**109.1.** If a card's text is written differently across several versions, the text of the latest released version of the card is generally considered the correct one.
**109.2.** When the rules or text mention a "card," it refers to a Duel Masters card.
* **109.2a** When choosing a card by an ability or effect, only the card itself is chosen. Even for an Evolution Creature composed of multiple cards or a shield with Shield Plus, only the specified card is directly affected.
* **109.2b** If a part of a creature composed of multiple cards leaves the Battle Zone, whether it leaves as a creature depends on its state at the time. For an Evolution Creature, the top card leaves the Battle Zone as a creature, but the underlying cards simply leave the Battle Zone as cards. A linked God leaves the Battle Zone as a creature, and a Psychic Linked creature leaves the Battle Zone as Psychic Cells.
* **109.2c** The effects and abilities of an element composed of multiple cards are applied to all cards included within it and expressing themselves as the element. Draghearts and Cross Gears are not included in a creature's component cards, but Auras, Magic Tools, etc., overlapping above or below the creature are included in its component cards. Also, Seals are not included in an element's component cards.
*Example: If you grant some ability to a linked God, the ability is applied to all linked Gods.*
*Example: When removing 《Mushura Degilmukade》 attached to 《Gorei, Will of the White Emperor》 with a card-choosing effect, you can specify and remove 《Mushura Degilmukade》.*

**110. Abilities and Effects**
**110.1.** An ability is a characteristic of a card that allows it to affect the game. A card's abilities are defined by its rule text. Abilities can also be granted to cards by rules or effects (granted by effects stating "has" or "gains"). Abilities generate effects.
**110.2.** Abilities may affect the card itself that has them, or they may affect other cards and players.
* **110.2a** Abilities are not only beneficial, but can also be detrimental. (Example: The text "This creature cannot attack" is an ability.)
* **110.2b** The conditions required to execute a card are the card's abilities.
* **110.2c** A single card may have multiple abilities. Each paragraph in the card's text starting with ■ represents a separate ability. Cards can also be given abilities by spells or other effects. If a single card has multiple instances of the same ability, they function independently. Depending on the ability, this might generate more effects than having just one instance, or it might not.
* **110.2d** Abilities generate one or more one-shot effects or continuous effects. Continuous effects include replacement effects.
* **110.2e** When an ability generates an effect that affects cards, it only affects the cards that are within its area of effect at the time the effect is generated. Cards that move into the affected zone later in the same turn are not affected by this effect.
**110.3.** Abilities are broadly divided into 4 types.
* **110.3a Spell Abilities:** These are the instructions processed between casting a spell and its resolution. A spell's rule text is its spell ability.
* **110.3b Triggered Abilities:** These typically have trigger conditions starting with "When...", "Whenever...", or "At...". Once triggered, the effect enters a standby state and waits to be resolved. A triggered ability granted by a spell or other effect is called a delayed triggered ability.
* **110.3c Activated Abilities:** Activated abilities trigger at a specific timing, and if the required conditions are met, you can choose to activate them. As a rule, they cannot be activated at just any arbitrary timing.
* **110.3d Static Abilities:** These generate a continuous effect that constantly functions as long as the creature, cross gear, weapon, fortress, heartbeat (Kodo) and field, aura, ritual (Gi), or nebula (Seiun) is in the Battle Zone, or as long as a castle or a creature with Shield Go is in the Shield Zone.
**110.4.** Basically, spell abilities function during the resolution of the spell, and other abilities function only while the card is in the Battle Zone or Shield Zone. However, there are several exceptions as listed below.
* **110.4a Characteristic-Defining Abilities:** These define the characteristics (cost, civilization, power, etc.) of the card itself. Abilities that increase or decrease characteristic values, such as Power Attacker or Sympathy, do not fall under this category. This ability functions in all zones, including outside the game. (Example: 《Nijisoku The Verde》, 《Super Divine Dragon Variant Bades》)
* **110.4b** Abilities that state which zone they function in function only in that zone. (Example: Mana Burst, Scorching Doron Go)
* **110.4c** Abilities that do not state which zone they function in function only in the Battle Zone. However, an ability of the card itself that grants it "S-Trigger" also functions while it is in the Shield Zone.
* **110.4d** Card abilities that allow it to be executed without paying its mana cost, without specifying a zone, function in any zone from which that card can be executed. (Example: Ninja Strike can be used from the Mana Zone under situations where summoning from the Mana Zone is possible.)
* **110.4e** Abilities that impose limits or modifications on the execution of the card itself function while those cards are in the zone from which they are attempting to be executed (usually the hand). (Example: Sympathy)
* **110.4f State-Defining Effects:** These alter the state of a card as it is placed into a zone. These interrupt effect processing similarly to replacement effects, but they are not treated as replacement effects, and if multiple exist, they all apply. If a replacement effect prevents the card from actually being placed into that zone, the state-defining effect does nothing. (Example: 《Eternal Ryusei Kaiser》, 《Bind Shadow, Shadow of Bondage》, 《"Holy Dog of Super Senses"》, 《Perfect Galaxy, Spirit of Immortality》)
* **110.4g** Triggered abilities with a trigger condition that can only occur while the card is in a zone other than the Battle Zone function while they are in that zone where they can trigger. (Example: 《Black God Dragon Ghoul-Generaid》)
**110.5.** The source of an effect is the card with the ability that generated it. The source of a triggered effect in a standby state is the card with the triggered ability.
* **110.5a** Once triggered, the effect becomes a standby effect independent of its source. After that, even if the card with the ability that is the source of the effect moves to another zone, the effect does not disappear. However, note that some effects do not directly do something themselves, but instead instruct their source to do something. Even if the source is no longer in its original zone at the time of the check, the source performs that processing. (Example: When choosing opponent's creatures so that their total power is less than or equal to the power of the creature that is the source of the effect, if the source creature is no longer in the Battle Zone, it refers to its power right before it left the Battle Zone.)
**110.6.** Effects that define card characteristics are distinguished from abilities gained through effects. If a card's characteristics are defined by an effect, it does not count as gaining an ability. (Example: Civilization is a characteristic, so an effect that adds a civilization does not count as giving an ability.)
**110.7.** Some abilities exert their effects multiple times if multiple instances of the same ability are held. (Example: Slayer, Power Attacker, Sympathy, etc.)

**111. Timing**
**111.1.** Whenever any processing occurs, the turn player's processing is always prioritized.
**111.2.** Processing is either carried out automatically by the rules of the game or carried out by the players.
* **111.2a** Triggered abilities trigger at any time, even while executing a card or resolving an effect. The effect enters a standby state the moment it triggers.
* **111.2b** Static abilities continuously affect the game.
* **111.2c** Turn-based actions occur automatically when a specific step begins or ends. (Example: Untapping, Drawing, etc.)
* **111.2d** State-based actions occur automatically when specific conditions are met. These are processed with priority, even during the resolution of an ability. (Example: Fulfilling win/loss conditions, destroying a creature with 0 or less power, etc.)

**112. Costs**
**112.1.** Cards have a mana cost to execute them.
**112.2.** The mana cost indicates the amount of mana required to execute the card. This also includes the payment of the civilizations the card possesses.
* **112.2a** When paying a mana cost, you tap cards to fulfill all the identical civilizations indicated by the mana cost. When executing a card, you first tap one card in the Mana Zone per civilization to fulfill all the civilizations included in the mana cost. Furthermore, you tap cards in the Mana Zone to pay the remaining amount so that the total numerical value written on the mana symbols of the tapped cards equals the cost.
*Example: When paying the cost for a 7-mana multi-colored card with Fire, Darkness, and Nature, you must tap a card with Fire, a card with Darkness, and a card with Nature from the Mana Zone separately (1 card each), and then tap enough remaining cards to bring the total mana value to 7. Tapping one multi-colored card that has Fire, Darkness, and Nature does not fulfill all those civilizations; it is treated as providing only one of Fire, Darkness, or Nature.*
* **112.2b** The actual mana cost a player pays may increase or decrease due to effects. If the mana cost becomes less than the required number of civilizations due to a cost reduction effect, the payment of civilizations is prioritized. Any excess mana at this time disappears at the end of the payment.
* **112.2c** You cannot tap extra cards in the Mana Zone if the payment for civilizations and costs has already been properly completed.
* **112.2d** Mana costs paid for O-Drive are treated as additional costs.
**112.3.** There are several abilities that allow you to execute a card without paying its mana cost. Furthermore, abilities that execute at specific timings require a declaration of use.
* **112.3a** S-Trigger is an ability where, when a shield is added to your hand due to a break, etc., you can execute the card immediately without paying its cost by showing it to your opponent and declaring its use. If multiple cards with S-Trigger are added from shields to your hand, you show and declare the cards you will use the S-Trigger ability for. Once all declarations are finished, execute those cards one by one.
* **112.3b** S-Back is an ability where, while a card with this ability is in a zone where it can be executed (usually the hand), when the specified card is added from a shield to your hand, you can immediately execute it without paying the cost by discarding that specified card and declaring its use. 
* **112.3c** Ninja Strike is an ability that allows you to summon a creature without paying its cost if the specified number of cards in the Mana Zone is met, during the non-turn player's processing timing after a turn player's creature attacks, or during the turn player's processing timing after a non-turn player's creature blocks.
* **112.3d** Sabaki Z is an ability where, while a card with this ability is in a zone where it can be executed (usually the hand), when a card with "Emblem of Judgment" is added from a shield to your hand, you can immediately execute it without paying the cost by discarding that card and declaring its use.
* **112.3e** G-Zero is an ability that allows you to summon a creature or cast a spell without paying its cost under specified conditions.
* **112.3f** Attack Chance is an ability that allows you to cast a spell without paying its cost under specified conditions.
* **112.3g** Even when using other effects that state "you may execute without paying the cost," the acts of "summoning," "casting," or "generating" themselves are still taking place, so abilities that trigger from those acts will trigger.
**112.4.** Cards placed in the Hyperspatial Zone or Ultra GR Zone also have mana costs, but unless there is a special effect, you cannot execute these cards by paying mana.

**113. Shields**
**113.1.** Shields are placed face down in the Shield Zone. The number of shields for each player at the start of the game is 5.
**113.2.** Normally, a shield consists of 1 card, but through effects like Shield Plus, multiple cards may be stacked together. When counting the number of shields, a stacked pile of cards is counted as 1 shield. When counting the number of cards in the Shield Zone, you count each individual card, including those in stacks.
* **113.2a** When rearranging shields by an effect, each shield to which cards are redistributed remembers its position and number of cards. Cards that reference a shield, such as Castles or Shield Force, re-reference the shield redistributed to that original position.
**113.3.** The number of shields may increase or decrease due to some effects.
**113.4.** A shield can be fortified by a Castle.
**113.5.** Shields are broken by creature attacks or by abilities.
**113.6.** When a shield is added to the hand due to a break or an effect, the player can declare the use of "S-Trigger", "G-Strike", and "S-Back". After all these declarations are finished, the shield moves to the hand.
* **113.6a** Until the player finishes declaring their use, the shield being added to the hand enters a standby state. A shield in a standby state is physically in the Shield Zone, but it is not included in the number of shields in the Shield Zone, nor is it counted in the number of cards in the Shield Zone.
* **113.6b** There are abilities that grant "S-Trigger" to cards in the Shield Zone. These abilities refer to the state just before the shield enters the standby state to determine whether to grant "S-Trigger".
* **113.6c** There are abilities that grant "S-Trigger" to a card being added to the hand from the Shield Zone. These abilities refer to the state of the standby shield to determine whether to grant "S-Trigger".
* **113.6d** If a shield being added to the hand was exerting a continuous effect in the Shield Zone, that effect continues until just before the declarations of "S-Trigger" etc. are finished and the shield moves to the hand.

**114. Drawing Cards**
**114.1.** For a player to draw a card means to put the top card of their deck into their hand. This is performed as a turn-based action during each player's Draw Step, or as an effect of a spell or ability.
**114.2.** Only 1 card can be drawn at an exact same time. If instructed to draw multiple cards, the player repeats drawing 1 card that number of times.
* **114.2a** If an effect causes multiple players to draw cards, first the turn player draws the specified number of cards, and then the non-turn player draws their cards.
**114.3.** If an effect moves a card from a player's deck to their hand without using the word "draw," that player has not drawn that card.
**114.4.** There are effects that replace drawing a card.
* **114.4a** If an effect replaces one of the draws when multiple cards are drawn, the replacement effect is completely processed before drawing the next card.
* **114.4b** There are effects that perform some additional processing on a card after it has been drawn. If drawing a card is replaced and as a result no card is drawn, that additional processing is not performed.
**114.5** If there are effects that trigger when a card is drawn, resolve them. Then, add it to your hand.
**114.6** Even when there are no cards in the deck, a player can attempt to draw a card. However, if there are no cards to draw in the deck, normally nothing happens.
* **114.6a** Attempting to draw a card when the deck is empty does not satisfy trigger conditions (like "When you draw a card"). You cannot attempt to draw a card to pay a cost. However, a replacement effect for drawing a card can still be applied when you attempt to draw a card while the deck is empty.

**115. Battle**
**115.1.** Comparing the power between creatures is called a battle.
* **115.1a** Battles are generated by creature attacks, or by abilities and effects.
**115.2.** When a battle begins, first, any abilities that trigger from it trigger. The triggered effects are processed in order, starting with the turn player.
**115.3.** Compare the power of both creatures.
* **115.3a** At this point, if either creature has been destroyed or removed from the combat, the battle does not take place. The power is compared only if both creatures are in the Battle Zone.
* **115.3b** The creature with the higher power wins the battle, and the creature with the lower power loses the battle. If they have the same power, both lose the battle. A creature that loses a battle is destroyed and placed in the graveyard (This is a state-based action).
* **115.3c** A creature with an ability that says "wins battles" wins the battle regardless of power, and the opposing creature loses. If both have an ability to win battles, they both win the battle, and neither creature loses.
* **115.3d** After that, abilities triggered by the result of the battle trigger. The triggered effects are processed in order, starting with the turn player.

**116. Seals**
**116.1.** A seal is placed face down on top of a card in the Battle Zone.
* **116.1a** To attach a seal, move the top card of the owner's deck face down on top of the card.
* **116.1b** It is possible to place multiple seals on a single card simultaneously.
**116.2.** A creature with a seal attached is ignored.
* **116.2a** An ignored creature is treated as if it does not exist in the Battle Zone. It cannot attack or block, exerts no abilities, cannot be chosen, and neither taps nor untaps.
**116.3.** Seals exist in the Battle Zone.
* **116.3a** When choosing a card in the Battle Zone by an effect, a seal can be chosen.
**116.4.** Seals are removed when a Command enters the Battle Zone.
* **116.4a** When a Command enters the Battle Zone, the owner of that Command places one seal into the graveyard from among the cards with seals attached that have the same civilization as the Command. You cannot choose not to remove one.
* **116.4b** Even though a creature with a seal attached is ignored, its civilizations are referenced when removing a seal. This is a state-based action.

**117. Hyperspatial Zone**
**117.1.** Psychics, Draghearts, Rules Plus, and Duel Mates placed in any zone other than the Battle Zone are placed in the Hyperspatial Zone. This movement cannot be prevented by card effects.

**118. Ultra GR Zone**
**118.1.** A GR Creature placed in any zone other than the Battle Zone is placed at the bottom of the Ultra GR Zone.
**118.2.** Only GR Creatures can exist in the Ultra GR Zone. If a normal card is placed into the Ultra GR Zone, it is put into the graveyard.

## 2. How to Read a Card

**200. General**
**200.1.** A card contains information necessary during the game, as well as information that has no particular meaning during the game.
**200.2.** Certain parts of a card are its "characteristics" (information referenced during the game that has mechanical meaning). Characteristics include civilization, cost, card name, race, card type, and power.
**200.3.** Characteristics may sometimes be ignored.
* **200.3a** Cards placed under another card due to evolution or similar mechanics generally have their characteristics ignored during normal gameplay. However, when an effect specifically specifies a "card", those characteristics are referenced. If an effect specifies something other than a card (like a creature), they are not referenced.
  * *Example: A Water creature that evolved on top of a Light and Water creature is just a Water creature, and its power and race are not affected by the card underneath. However, if affected by "Tech-Dan's Wave Destruction Go!", the cost of the underlying card is also referenced, and if it is valid, the card underneath receives the effect.*
  * *Example: The "Gates of No Moon - Zetsu" ability of 《卍getsu Ga Ryuzark 卍》 references Magic Tools, so it cannot reference the cards underneath 《卍 De Suzark 卍》.*
* **200.3b** The characteristics of a Seal cannot be verified. If a Seal is specified as a card, its cost is treated as 0, and it is treated as a card with no other characteristics.
* **200.3c** When specifying a double-sided card as a card, the face-up side is referenced.

**201. Cost**
**201.1.** Cost is a characteristic a card possesses. It is normally indicated by the number in the upper left corner of the card.
* **201.1a** The numerical value of a card's mana cost represents the amount of mana required to pay to execute that card.
* **201.1b** Psychics and Draghearts have mana costs, but unless there is a special effect, they cannot be executed by paying mana from the Hyperspatial Zone.
**201.2.** When referencing a card's cost, reference that numerical value.
* **201.2a** Even if the cost to execute a card is altered by an effect, when referencing the card's cost, the cost originally printed on the card is referenced.
  * *Example: Even if 《Cocco Lupia》 is in play, the cost of a 《Bolshack NEX》 in the Battle Zone is 6, and it will not be destroyed by the effect of 《Hyperspatial Musha Hole》 which destroys a creature costing 4 or less.*
* **201.2b** There are cards like Twinpacts that have 2 costs. When referencing the cost of a card with 2 costs by an effect or ability, the player who used that effect or ability references only the cost of their choice.
  * *Example: If the player who cast 《7777777》 chooses a Twinpact card revealed from the top of the opponent's deck, the player who cast 《7777777》 chooses which cost to reference.*
* **201.2c** If a card with 2 costs like a Twinpact is revealed during a Gachinko Judge, the player who revealed the card (not the player who used the ability) decides which cost to reference. This is an exception to 201.2b.

**202. Card Name**
**202.1.** Card Name is a characteristic of a card. It is normally printed at the top center of the card.
**202.2.** When an effect instructs a player to "specify 1 card name," that player must choose a card name that actually exists. For cards with variations sharing a part of the name, the full name must be declared. (However, in actual gameplay, an abbreviation mutually understood by both players is acceptable.)
**202.3.** Some cards reference a part of a card name. In that case, it references all cards that include the specified name in their card name.
  * *Example: 《Bolshack NEX》 references all cards containing the word "Lupia" in their card name. If a word is interrupted or split, like in "Purple Pierce", it is not considered a matching word.*
* **202.3a** When referencing a card name, it must perfectly match, including the ruby characters (furigana reading).
**202.4.** Some cards add another card name to their original card name. In that case, the card possesses two card names simultaneously.
**202.5.** When Gods are linked, that God possesses the card names of all the linked cards.
  * *Example: If a "linked 《Dragon God Heavy》 and 《Dragon God Metal》" and a "linked 《Dragon God Heavy》 and 《Destruction God Death》" are in the Battle Zone simultaneously, both of these linked Gods have the name 《Dragon God Heavy》. Under these circumstances, if 《Tomb of Angels and Demons》 is cast, both are destroyed because they are both creatures with 《Dragon God Heavy》 in their name.*
**202.6.** Some promotional or alternate-art versions of cards have an alias line just below where the regular card name is printed. For game and deck-building purposes, these cards are always treated exclusively as having the card name written on the alias line.
  * *Example: The Transformers collaboration card 《Optimus Prime》 is always treated only as 《"Bad Punish" Brand》 in terms of gameplay and deck building.*
* **202.6a** During a game, when specifying a creature's name or card name by an effect or rule, you cannot specify the card name located above the alias line on a card that has an alias.
  * *Example: For abilities that specify the name of a creature or card, such as those possessed by 《Holy Dragon Emotional Hardcore》, you cannot specify 《Optimus Prime》 or 《Bumblebee》.*

**203. Race**
**203.1.** Race is a characteristic of a card. It is normally printed immediately below the card name.
**203.2.** Some effects reference a race or a part of a race. In that case, it references all cards that include the specified name in their race.
  * *Example: An effect that specifies "Dragon" references all cards that contain "Dragon" in their race.*
**203.3.** A race may be added by an effect.
**203.4.** When choosing a race for an effect, you cannot choose a part of a race or a race that does not exist standalone.
  * *Example: "Cyber" is a race that does not exist standalone, so it cannot be chosen.*

**204. Card Type**
**204.1.** Card type is a characteristic of a card. It is normally printed immediately below the illustration.
**204.2.** Card types include "Creature," "Spell," "Cross Gear," "Castle," "Cell," "Weapon," "Fortress," "Heartbeat" (Kodo), "Field," "Core," "Aura," "Ritual" (Gi), "Nebula" (Seiun), "Artifact," "Land," "Rule Plus," "Tamaseed," and "Duelist."
**204.3.** Cards may have a supertype (special type). These are written just before the card type. Supertypes include "Evolution," "Psychic," "Psychic Super," "Exile," "Dragheart," "Forbidden," "D2," "NEO," "GR," "Star Evolution," etc.
* **204.3a** "Creatures" can have supertypes such as "Evolution," "Psychic," "Psychic Super," "Exile," "Dragheart," "Forbidden," "NEO," "GR," and "Star Evolution."
* **204.3b** "Heartbeats" can have the "Forbidden" supertype.
* **204.3c** "Fields" can have supertypes such as "D2" and "Final Forbidden."

**205. Text Box**
**205.1.** The text box is located in the lower half of the card. In most cases, the rule text defining the card's abilities is written here.
* **205.1a** For keyword abilities, the keyword may precede the text. When referencing the text, skip the keyword part and do not reference it.
  * *Example: When your 《Black Bean Baron/White Rice Baron》 is in the Battle Zone, if an opponent's creature with Just Diver enters the Battle Zone, that creature is put into the Mana Zone.*
**205.2.** Text boxes sometimes contain text that has no in-game meaning.
* **205.2a** Reminder text is text enclosed in parentheses that summarizes the rules applied to that card. It is usually written on the same line as the corresponding ability, but for reminder text covering the whole card rather than a specific ability, it may be written on an independent line.
* **205.2b** Flavor text has no meaning during the game.
**205.3.** The symbol in the background of the text box has no meaning during the game.

**206. Power**
**206.1.** Power is a characteristic of a card. The number written in the bottom left of a creature card typically represents its power. Power may receive modifications or be changed to a specific value by effects.
**206.2.** On some creature cards, a "+" or "-" is written after the power. This indicates that the creature's power fluctuates due to an ability it possesses itself.
* **206.2a** In any zone other than the Battle Zone, these creatures are treated as having the base numerical power before the "+" or "-" modification is applied.
**206.3.** Some cards have effects that fix a creature's power. As long as this effect is applied, no other effect whatsoever can change this numerical value, and the power remains fixed even if the individual creature's state changes.
  * *Example: In a situation where an effect fixes the power of all creatures in the Battle Zone to 500, if 2 or more creatures are linked, their power is not combined; the power of the linked creature remains fixed at 500.*
**206.4.** Some cards have effects that double a creature's power. This effect is applied after applying all other power increases/decreases.
  * *Example: If there is a creature with 3000 power, and an effect grants power +2000 along with an effect that doubles power simultaneously, it first becomes 5000 (3000 + 2000), and then doubled to 10000 power.*

**207. Mana Symbol**
**207.1.** Except for Psychics, Draghearts, and some Forbiddens, a mana symbol and a number are printed at the bottom center of the card. The mana symbol represents the card's civilization, and the number on the mana symbol represents the amount of mana it can generate when tapped while in the Mana Zone.
**207.2.** The numerical value on the mana symbol may be changed by effects.
**207.3.** The mana symbol on a colorless card does not represent a civilization.

**208. Other Information Groups**
**208.1.** Cards also contain other information that has no meaning during a normal game.
* **208.1a** Illustrations, illustrator names, rarity symbols, expansion symbols, expansion codes, block symbols, copyright text, collector numbers, frames, borders, tabs, and icons typically have no direct impact on the game, but they may be referenced by certain effects.
  * *Example: Soul Icon, H.C. Icon, Victory Icon, etc.*

## 3. Card Types

**300. General**
**300.1.** Card types include "Creature," "Spell," "Cross Gear," "Castle," "Cell," "Weapon," "Fortress," "Heartbeat," "Field," "Core," "Aura," "Ritual," "Nebula," "Artifact," "Land," "Rule Plus," "Tamaseed," and "Duelist."

**301. Creature**
**301.1.** The turn player can summon a creature from their hand during their Main Step.
**301.2.** When summoned, a creature enters the Battle Zone.
**301.3.** Power is a characteristic possessed only by creatures.
* **301.3a** A creature's power represents its strength in battle.
* **301.3b** To determine a creature's power, calculate from the base value printed in the bottom left of the card and apply any various continuous effects.
**301.4.** Creatures can attack.
**301.5.** A creature cannot attack unless it has been in the Battle Zone continuously since the beginning of the player's turn. This rule is called "Summoning Sickness."

**302. Spell**
**302.1.** The turn player can cast a spell from their hand during their Main Step.
**302.2.** Once a spell is cast, perform the processing written in its rule text in the order written, and afterward it is put into the graveyard.
**302.3.** A spell never exists standalone in the Battle Zone. If it is placed there improperly, it is put into the graveyard.

**303. Cross Gear**
**303.1.** The turn player can generate a Cross Gear from their hand during their Main Step.
**303.2.** When generated, a Cross Gear enters the Battle Zone.
**303.3.** A Cross Gear can cross onto a creature. It cannot cross onto non-creature cards.
* **303.3a** A creature that has a Cross Gear attached is referred to as a "crossed creature." The Cross Gear is "crossed" onto the creature.
* **303.3b** To cross a Cross Gear onto a creature, you must pay its mana cost again. This ability is an activated ability normally usable only during the Main Step.
* **303.3c** A Cross Gear may be crossed onto a creature by a spell or other ability.
* **303.3d** The orientation (tapped/untapped) of a Cross Gear does not depend on the state of the creature it is crossed with.
**303.4.** A Cross Gear that is already crossed onto a creature can be re-crossed onto another creature.
* **303.4a** You cannot re-cross a Cross Gear onto the exact same creature it is currently crossed with.
**303.5.** A Cross Gear remains in the Battle Zone even if the creature it is crossed with leaves the Battle Zone.

**304. Castle**
**304.1.** The turn player can fortify a Castle from their hand during their Main Step.
**304.2.** A Castle enters the Shield Zone and fortifies by being attached to a shield.
* **304.2a** A shield with a Castle attached is said to be "fortified," and the Castle is said to be "fortifying" the shield.
**304.3.** If a fortified shield leaves the Shield Zone, after resolving any S-Trigger abilities the shield may have had, the Castle is put into the graveyard.

**305. Weapon**
**305.1.** The only Weapons that currently exist are Dragheart Weapons, which can only enter the Battle Zone via effects that put Draghearts into the Battle Zone.
**305.2.** A Weapon can be equipped to a creature.
* **305.2a** The orientation of a Weapon does not depend on the state of the equipped creature.
**305.3.** A Weapon cannot exist standalone in the Battle Zone.

**306. Fortress**
**306.1.** The only Fortresses that currently exist are Dragheart Fortresses, which can only enter the Battle Zone via effects that put Draghearts into the Battle Zone.
**306.2.** A Fortress can exist standalone in the Battle Zone.
**306.3.** The correct upright position (untapped state) for a Fortress is horizontal.

**307. Heartbeat (Kodo)**
**307.1.** A Heartbeat is one face of a double-sided "Forbidden" card that can be put in a deck.
**307.2.** Some Heartbeats are placed in a specified position at the start of the game.

**308. Field**
**308.1.** The turn player can deploy a Field from their hand during their Main Step.
**308.2.** When deployed, a Field enters the Battle Zone.
**308.3.** The correct upright position (untapped state) for a Field is horizontal. If a "D Switch" has already been used, its upside-down position is treated as the untapped state.
**308.4.** Some Fields are placed in a specified position at the start of the game.

**309. Core**
**309.1.** The only Core that currently exists is the Forbidden Core, which is placed in the Battle Zone only by flipping a Final Forbidden Field and combining it as 1 creature.
**309.2.** A Core has no means of entering the Battle Zone standalone.
**309.3.** The correct upright position (untapped state) for a Core is vertical. Upside-down ones also exist.
**309.4.** A Core moves to the location specified as a card.
**309.5.** A Core can be placed in the Mana Zone on either side. In either case, it cannot produce mana. It can be tapped as a card of its civilization.
**309.6.** A Core may be stacked underneath a creature.
**309.7.** A Core cannot exist standalone in the Battle Zone. If it becomes standalone, it is immediately put into the graveyard.
**309.8.** A Forbidden Core by itself has no cost. When referenced, its cost is treated as 0.

**310. Aura**
**310.1.** The turn player can execute an Aura from their hand during their Main Step.
**310.2.** When an Aura is executed, it is attached to a GR Creature, or you GR Summon a GR Creature and put it into the Battle Zone, then attach the Aura to it.
* **310.2a** If the target GR Creature leaves the Battle Zone after the Aura is executed but before it is attached, the Aura does not enter the Battle Zone and remains in the zone it was in when executed.
  * *Example: You execute an Aura from your hand and GR Summon 《Yattare Robo》, but if 《Gruesome Fang, Paranormal》 destroys it with a static ability, the Aura does not enter the Battle Zone and remains in your hand.*
**310.3.** The correct upright position (untapped state) for an Aura is horizontal.
**310.4.** The number in the bottom left of an Aura is not its power. While attached to a creature, the Aura increases the power of the attached creature by that amount.
**310.5.** An Aura cannot exist standalone in the Battle Zone. If placed improperly, it goes to the graveyard.
**310.6.** When attaching an Aura to a GR Creature that already has an Aura attached, place it on top of the most recently attached Aura.
**310.7.** If a GR Creature with an Aura evolves and is no longer a GR Creature, the Aura remains in the Battle Zone attached to that creature, and the Aura's power and abilities apply to that creature.
* **310.7a** In this case, the Evolution Creature is placed directly below the Aura, stacking from top to bottom: Aura, Evolution Creature, GR Creature.
**310.8.** If a creature with an Aura moves from the Battle Zone to another zone, the attached Aura also moves to that destination zone as a component card of the creature.
  * *Example: If a creature with an Aura attached is chosen by 《Soul-Piercing Justice of the Emperor Z》, the attached Aura is placed face-up on the same shield as the creature.*
  * *Example: If a creature with an Aura is chosen by the "When this enters" ability of 《Natura Scorpion》, the attached Aura is also placed in the Mana Zone in a tapped state.*
* **310.8a** During the movement, the Aura and the creature are placed in the zone simultaneously. For GR Creatures, after the Aura and GR Creature move simultaneously, the GR Creature returns to the Ultra GR Zone.
* **310.8b** If a creature with an Aura attached is placed under another card in the Battle Zone, the attached Aura is also placed under that card. In this case, the Aura and the creature are placed under the card in whichever order the owner chooses.

**311. Ritual (Gi), Nebula (Seiun)**
**311.1.** The only Ritual that currently exists is Ritual of Zerom, and the only Nebula is Nebula of Zerom.
**311.2.** Rituals of Zerom and Nebulas of Zerom are placed in specified positions at the start of the game.
**311.3.** Rituals of Zerom and Nebulas of Zerom have no means of entering the Battle Zone standalone.

**312. Artifact**
**312.1.** The turn player can execute an Artifact from their hand during their Main Step.
**312.2.** When executed, an Artifact enters the Battle Zone. *(Note: rule document incorrectly numbers this as 311.2, but the sequence implies 312.2)*

**313. Land**
**313.1.** Lands cannot enter the Battle Zone by normal methods and cannot be executed.
**313.2.** Lands have effects that exert themselves while in the Mana Zone.
**313.3.** Lands cannot exist in the Battle Zone. If placed in the Battle Zone by any means, they are immediately put into the graveyard.

**314. Rule Plus**
**314.1.** Rule Plus cards are placed in the Hyperspatial Zone at the start of the game.
**314.2.** Rule Plus cards cannot be put into the Battle Zone by normal methods.
**314.3.** A Rule Plus card exerts its effect when starting the game by placing it in the Hyperspatial Zone, adding additional rules to the game.
* **314.3a** If the effect of a Rule Plus used in a game alters the number of cards required to be used in the game, you must prepare cards to fulfill the modified required number before the game starts.
  * *Example: If you want to start the game with 1 copy of 《The 13th Plan》 placed in the Hyperspatial Zone, you must prepare a deck of exactly 45 cards in advance.*
**314.4.** Rule Plus cards cannot exist in the Battle Zone. If placed in the Battle Zone by any means, they pass through the graveyard and immediately return to the Hyperspatial Zone.

**315. Tamaseed**
**315.1.** The turn player can execute a Tamaseed from their hand during their Main Step.
**315.2.** When executed, a Tamaseed enters the Battle Zone.

**316. Element**
**316.1.** Element is a collective term for card types like Creatures and Tamaseeds that can be put into the Battle Zone face up.
**316.2.** Card types that cannot enter the Battle Zone standalone, such as Spells, Castles, and Cores, are not included in Elements.
**316.3.** An effect that specifies an Element is not an effect that specifies a card. Therefore, if an Element composed of multiple cards is specified, all cards constituting that Element are affected.
  * *Example: If an Evolution Creature is chosen by an effect that says "Choose 1 element and destroy it", the underlying cards are also put into the graveyard together with it, just as when chosen by an effect that says "Destroy a creature".*

**317. Duelist**
**317.1.** The turn player can execute a Duelist from their hand during their Main Step.
**317.2.** When executed, a Duelist enters the Battle Zone.
**317.3.** While a Duelist is in the Battle Zone, its owner cannot put a Duelist with the same name into the Battle Zone.
**317.4.** Some Duelists possess the ability to execute Duel Mate cards from the Hyperspatial Zone without paying the cost. Normally, this ability can only be used during your Main Step.
  * *Example: 《Stein, Silent Guardian》 has "Duelist: Stein". With this ability, once per turn, during your Main Step, you can execute 1 Duel Mate card with Stein in its race without paying its cost.*
**317.5.** Some Duelists have a "Duelist Link" ability that flips multiple Duel Mate cards and links them together as 1 creature. This is a static ability.
  * *Example: In a situation where 《"Restarting an Ended Story is Fruitless"》 and 《"Be Merely a Quietly Spoken Existence"》 are placed under 《Stein, Silent Guardian》 in the Battle Zone, when you put out 《Magao》 via "Duelist: Stein", before resolving 《Magao》's "When this enters" ability, you flip the 3 Duel Mate cards and link them as 《Sherlock Altamont, The Final Name》.*

## 4. Zones

**400. General**
**400.1.** A zone is a place where cards can exist during a game. Normally, there are 9 zones: "Deck", "Hand", "Battle Zone", "Graveyard", "Mana Zone", "Shield Zone", "Hyperspatial Zone", "Ultra GR Zone", and "Abyss Zone". The Battle Zone is shared between players, but the other zones exist individually for each player and are not shared.
**400.2.** All zones are categorized as either Public Zones or Private Zones.
* **400.2a** A Public Zone is a zone where all players can see cards that are not face down. The Battle Zone, Mana Zone, Graveyard, Hyperspatial Zone, and Abyss Zone are Public Zones.
* **400.2b** A Private Zone is a zone where not all players can inherently see the faces of the cards. The Shield Zone, Deck, Hand, and Ultra GR Zone are Private Zones. Even if the faces of all cards in that zone are revealed, the zone itself remains a Private Zone.
* **400.2c** When an effect requires each player to choose a card from a Private Zone, the card remains face down at the time it is chosen. However, it must be clearly indicated which card was chosen.
* **400.2d** When using an ability that references cards in a Private Zone, if the referenced card cannot be found, there is no need to reveal that Private Zone to the opponent to prove it.
  * *Example: When using the ability of 《Kaiser "Baki", Apex of Victory》 to put 5 Hunters into the Battle Zone, even if you did not have any Hunters in your hand to put out, you do not need to reveal your hand.*
* **400.2e** When a card is added to a Private Zone, unless specified otherwise, it is added while maintaining a private (face-down) state.
* **400.2f** When a card is added from a Private Zone to a Public Zone, unless specified otherwise, the card is revealed after moving zones. However, if there is a need to reference the text of the moving card due to a replacement effect or similar, all players verify the face of the card just before it moves. The same applies when a card placed in a private state within a Public Zone is added to a Public Zone. This is not treated as an "turn face up" effect in terms of game mechanics.
  * *Example: In a situation where you have Water and Fire civilizations in your Mana Zone, when you attempt to place 《Yoroshiku Carol》 from your hand into the Graveyard, before the card is actually placed in the Graveyard, all players verify that it is 《Yoroshiku Carol》 and apply the replacement effect. As a result, 《Yoroshiku Carol》 is not placed in the Graveyard and remains in your hand.*
**400.3.** Depending on the card type, there are zones that certain cards cannot enter.
**400.4.** The order of cards in the Shield Zone and Deck cannot be rearranged unless instructed by an effect or a rule. Cards in other zones can be freely rearranged by their owner as they wish. However, whether they are tapped or not, and what cards are attached to them, must remain clear to all players.
**400.5.** When a card moves from one zone to another, it is treated as newly added to that zone and does not retain the state it had in the previous zone.
* **400.5a** A card that moved from a Public Zone to a Private Zone can no longer be seen.
* **400.5b** When a tapped card in the Mana Zone is moved to the Battle Zone, it enters the Battle Zone in an untapped state.
**400.6.** There are effects that instruct a player to do something to one of the zones (e.g., "Shuffle your hand into your deck"). These processes affect all cards currently in that zone, but the zone itself is not affected.
**400.7.** When multiple cards move simultaneously to any zone other than the Battle Zone, they all move at the exact same time.
**400.8.** When an effect is processed spanning multiple zones, if the target moves to a zone outside the effect's specification midway through, the effect performs no further processing on it.

**401. Deck**
**401.1.** When the game begins, each player's deck becomes their deck (mountain/library).
**401.2.** Each deck must be a single, face-down pile. Players must not peek at the cards in the deck or alter their order.
* **401.2a** When searching the deck for a card due to an effect, you must not alter the order of the cards until you are instructed to shuffle it afterward.
**401.3.** A player may count the number of cards remaining in their opponent's deck at any time.
**401.4.** When an effect simultaneously places multiple cards on the top or bottom of the same deck, unless specified otherwise, the owner of those cards can determine the order in which they are placed. They do not have to reveal to the opponent what order they were placed in.
**401.5.** There are effects that instruct a player to reveal the top card of the deck. The revealed card returns to being face down at the stage when the effect's processing is completed.
**401.6.** When an effect instructs a player to place a card "Nth from the top" of the deck, if that deck has less than N cards, that player places the card at the very bottom of that deck.
**401.7.** When an effect adds cards to the deck in bulk, it is not considered to have been added to the "top" or "bottom" of the deck.

**402. Hand**
**402.1.** The hand is the place where a player holds drawn cards. Cards may also move to the hand from zones other than the deck due to other effects. At the start of the game, each player draws 5 cards.
**402.2.** There is no maximum hand size limit for either player.
**402.3.** A player can arrange their hand conveniently and look at it whenever they like. You cannot look at other players' hands, but you can count the number of cards in them at any time.

**403. Battle Zone**
**403.1.** The Battle Zone is the main area of the game. Creatures, Cross Gears, Weapons, Fortresses, Heartbeats, Fields, Auras, Rituals, and Nebulas are placed in the Battle Zone, but mana, shields, and spells are never placed here. Effects that affect or reference Elements only affect and reference Elements in the Battle Zone, unless other zones are written in that text.
  * *Example: An effect saying "Choose 1 element and destroy it" can only choose an element currently in the Battle Zone.*
  * *Example 2: An effect saying "Choose 1 creature and return it to its owner's hand" can only choose a creature currently in the Battle Zone.*
**403.2.** Players must arrange the creatures in the Battle Zone so that each is clearly distinct, and it must be made clear which creature a Cross Gear or Weapon is attached to.
**403.3.** A player may rearrange their own cards in the Battle Zone as they like at any time.
**403.4.** The order of cards stacked underneath a card cannot be changed. This is an exception to 403.3.
* **403.4a** When placing a card underneath a card in the Battle Zone, it is placed at the very bottom of its component cards. When multiple cards are placed under a card at once, the player determines the order in which to place them.

**404. Graveyard**
**404.1.** The Graveyard is the place to put discarded cards. Cards discarded from the hand, destroyed cards, resolved spells, etc., are placed in the Graveyard.
**404.2.** A player can look at the cards in the Graveyard at any time.
**404.3.** A player can rearrange the cards in their own Graveyard as they like at any time.

**405. Mana Zone**
**405.1.** The Mana Zone is the place to put cards that generate the mana needed to execute cards. All cards placed in the Mana Zone are placed upside down. However, multi-colored cards are placed tapped.
**405.2.** A player can look at the cards in the Mana Zone at any time.
**405.3.** A player can rearrange the cards in their own Mana Zone as they like at any time.
**405.4.** Cards in the Mana Zone are normally face up, but they may become face down due to effects. A face-down card is treated as a card with a cost of 0, colorless, a mana symbol number of 1, and no other characteristics. Also, while face down, the information on the front face cannot be referenced.
**405.5.** A player can look at the front face of a face-down card in their own Mana Zone at any time. However, they cannot look at the front face of a face-down card in their opponent's Mana Zone.

**406. Shield Zone**
**406.1.** At the start of the game, 5 shields are placed in the Shield Zone. Castles that fortify shields are placed in the Shield Zone.
**406.2.** Each shield must be a single, face-down pile of cards. Players cannot look at the contents of their respective shields.
**406.3.** Players cannot rearrange the cards in the Shield Zone.

**407. Hyperspatial Zone**
**407.1.** Cards with the special types "Psychic" and "Dragheart" are placed in the Hyperspatial Zone. The maximum number of cards in the Hyperspatial Zone at the start of the game is 8. It can also be 0 cards.
**407.2.** A player can look at the cards in the Hyperspatial Zone at any time.
**407.3.** A player can rearrange the cards in their own Hyperspatial Zone as they like at any time.

**408. Ultra GR Zone**
**408.1.** Cards with the special type "GR" are placed in the Ultra GR Zone. The number of Ultra GR cards at the start of the game must be exactly 12.
**408.2.** The Ultra GR must be a face-down pile of cards. Players cannot look at the contents of the Ultra GR.
**408.3.** Players cannot rearrange the cards in the Ultra GR Zone.

**409. Pending State (保留状態)**
**409.1.** When a card is executed, or when a card is about to enter the Battle Zone due to an effect, those cards enter a "pending state" until they actually move zones. Even if a card enters a pending state, it does not mean it has left that zone.
* **409.1a** When executing a card like a creature that enters the Battle Zone, or when attempting to put it into the Battle Zone by an effect, those cards enter a pending state until they actually move zones.
* **409.1b** A spell enters a pending state from the time it is cast until its effect finishes resolving. After resolution is complete, the cast spell is placed in the Graveyard.
* **409.1c** While a card is in a pending state, it still belongs to the zone it was originally placed in, but it is not counted towards the number of cards in that zone.
  * *Example: In a situation where 《Crimson God Dragon Ogristval》 is in play, if you cast the last remaining 《Emergency Typhoon》 in your hand, before resolving the card-drawing effect, 《Crimson God Dragon Ogristval》's power becomes 0 and it is destroyed.*
  * *Example 2: When discarding a card from your hand via the effect of 《Emergency Typhoon》 cast from your hand, you cannot discard the 《Emergency Typhoon》 itself that you just cast.*
  * *Example 3: In a situation where you have 4 cards in your Mana Zone and the opponent has 《Beast Army Yadokku》 in the Battle Zone, when summoning 《Nijisoku The Verde》 from your Mana Zone, 《Nijisoku The Verde》 enters a pending state, making the number of cards in your Mana Zone 3. Thus, the replacement effect of 《Beast Army Yadokku》 is applied.*
* **409.1d** After a card that was in a pending state is no longer in a pending state, that card loses the effects that were granted to it before it became pending.
  * *Example: If you execute a card from your Graveyard that was granted the effect "During this turn, you may execute this from your Graveyard," and as a result, the card is placed in the Graveyard again, you cannot execute it a second time.*
  * *Example 2: When summoning a creature granted the effect of 《Boss Goemonkey, Snake Hand!》 from the Mana Zone, if that creature remains in the Mana Zone due to the replacement effect of 《Beast Army Yadokku》, you cannot summon that creature from the Mana Zone again.*
* **409.1e** Multiple cards may be executed simultaneously by a single effect. In that case, all cards to be executed enter a pending state first, and then their processing is performed one by one.
  * *Example: When casting 3 spells via the ability of 《Type-A Dragon Emperor Kiriko³》, all 3 enter a pending state, and then you resolve them one by one.*
  * *Example 2: When 7 cards are placed in the Graveyard via the ability of 《Dueland, Dream Transformation》, all the cards to be executed enter a pending state first, and then you execute them one by one.*
* **409.1f** Multiple cards may enter the Battle Zone simultaneously by a single effect. In that case, all cards entering the Battle Zone enter a pending state first, and then they enter the Battle Zone one by one. However, if multiple seals enter the Battle Zone simultaneously, they all enter a pending state first, and then are attached to creatures in the Battle Zone simultaneously.
  * *Example: When putting 2 creatures into the Battle Zone via the effect of 《Heaven's Gate》, those 2 enter a pending state, then enter the Battle Zone one by one.*
  * *Example 2: When a seal is attached to all creatures in the Battle Zone via the effect of 《Dokindam Apocalypse》, they are attached simultaneously.*
**409.2.** Even if a card that entered a pending state as a result of being executed or having its effect resolved does not move to another zone, it still loses its pending state. The state of that card does not change from what it was before it entered the pending state.
* **409.2a** If a pending card is placed again into the zone it was originally placed in, it does not count as having been "placed" in that zone anew. Abilities that trigger when a card is placed do not trigger, and the orientation of that card does not change.
  * *Example: When you cast a spell from the Graveyard using the "When this enters" ability of 《Wave Weyb》, the cast spell is placed in the Graveyard again as a result of resolution, but abilities that trigger when a card is placed in the Graveyard do not trigger at this time.*
  * *Example 2: When summoning 《Dance of Yin-Yang》 that is placed tapped in the Mana Zone, if you apply the replacement effect of 《Beast Army Yadokku》, 《Dance of Yin-Yang》 is placed in the Mana Zone remaining in a tapped state even after the replacement effect is applied.*
* **409.2b** A replacement effect applied by a spell being placed in the Graveyard is also applied even if a spell cast from the Graveyard is placed in the Graveyard again. This is an exception to 409.2.
  * *Example: When casting 《Kenzan Charger》 from the Graveyard using the "When this enters" ability of 《Wave Weyb》, when 《Kenzan Charger》 is about to be placed in the Graveyard again, it is placed in the Mana Zone instead due to the Charger ability.*

**410. Abyss Zone**
**410.1.** Cards sent to the abyss are placed in the Abyss Zone.
**410.2.** A player can look at the cards in the Abyss Zone at any time.
**410.3.** A player can rearrange the cards in their own Abyss Zone as they like at any time.
**410.4.** A card placed in the Abyss Zone does not move to any zone other than the Abyss Zone during that game. This also applies to Psychics, Draghearts, and GR Creatures.
  * *Example: If 《Almighty Zenno》 is sent to the abyss, the 《Almighty Zenno》 placed in the Abyss Zone does not return to the Ultra GR Zone, but remains in the Abyss Zone.*

## 5. Turn Structure

**500. General**
**500.1.** A turn consists of 6 steps in the following order: "Start of Turn", "Draw", "Mana Charge", "Main", "Attack", and "End of Turn". Each step exists every turn, even if nothing happens. Each step may further have sub-steps. These are processed in order.
**500.2.** Each step ends when there are no effects currently resolving and the turn player has nothing left they want to do. Each sub-step during an attack proceeds to the next sub-step when both players have no further actions.
* **500.2a** Once you proceed to the next step, you cannot return to a previous step.
**500.3.** When a step or sub-step ends, any effects that were scheduled to last "until the end" of that step or sub-step expire. Also, any effects that were scheduled to last "until" a certain step or sub-step expire at the beginning of that step or sub-step. Effects that last "during an attack" expire when each creature's attack ends, not at the end of the Attack Step. Effects that last "during the turn" expire after the turn is completely finished, so they do not expire immediately upon entering the End of Turn step.
**500.4.** When a step or sub-step begins, triggered effects that trigger "at the beginning" of that step or sub-step enter a standby state.
**500.5.** Through effects, a player may gain additional turns. In that case, add the gained turn immediately after the current turn. If one player gains multiple additional turns during a single turn, those additional turns are added one by one.
* **500.5a** If each player gains an additional turn during a single turn, the additional turns are taken starting with the turn player.
**500.6.** The player going first skips the Draw Step of their first turn.

**501. Start of Turn Step**
**501.1.** The turn player decides which of their cards to untap, and untaps them simultaneously. This is a turn-based action. Normally, a player untaps all their cards, but effects may prevent cards from untapping.
* **501.1a** A player may choose not to untap a creature with the Silent Skill ability. This acts as a replacement for the untap due to the Silent Skill ability. However, the effect of the Silent Skill itself is independent of the activation condition. The effect of the Silent Skill enters a standby state the moment it is activated and is resolved after the other cards have untapped.
**501.2.** All abilities that trigger "at the beginning of the turn" trigger. The triggered effects are processed in order, after the turn player declares they will resolve them.
* **501.2a** If a card with an ability that triggers "at the beginning of the turn" moves to a zone where the ability can trigger during this step, that ability will not trigger until the Start of Turn Step of the next turn.

**502. Draw Step**
**502.1.** The turn player draws 1 card. This is a turn-based action.

**503. Mana Charge Step**
**503.1.** The turn player may place 1 card from their hand into the Mana Zone upside down (charge). They may choose to do this, or choose not to.
**503.2.** Normally, a player can only charge 1 mana during their Mana Charge Step. Through continuous effects, this number may increase or decrease.

**504. Main Step**
**504.1.** Normally, only during the Main Step can the turn player execute cards.
**504.2.** By paying the mana cost of a Cross Gear in the Battle Zone again, you can cross the Cross Gear onto a creature.

**505. Attack Step**
**505.1.** The Attack Step consists of 5 sub-steps in order: "Specify Attacking Creature", "Specify Blocking Creature", "Battle", "Direct Attack", and "End of Attack".
**505.2.** During the Attack Step, the turn player's creatures can attack the non-turn player or the non-turn player's tapped creatures.
**505.3.** Only creatures can attack or block.
**505.4.** Creatures attack and block one at a time. Multiple creatures cannot attack simultaneously, nor can multiple creatures block simultaneously.
**505.5.** The turn player can declare an attack as many times as they want, as long as they have creatures capable of attacking.
**505.6.** If an attacking creature or a blocking creature leaves the Battle Zone, it is treated as no longer participating in "that attack." Some effects may also remove a creature from "that attack." A creature removed from "that attack" is no longer the attacking creature, nor the blocking creature, nor the blocked creature, nor the unblocked creature.
* **505.6a** Once a creature is specified as an attacking creature or a blocking creature, even if an ability prohibiting it from participating in an attack or block activates afterward, it is not removed from the current attack or block. However, if the attacking creature or the creature being attacked is removed from "that attack" by some effect, the attack immediately ends, and you move to the End of Attack Step. At this time, effects already waiting to resolve will resolve, but you cannot newly add abilities that the non-turn player can optionally declare to use (as defined in 506.3d).
* **505.6b** You cannot remove a creature already specified as an attacking or blocking creature from the attack by tapping or untapping it.

**506. Specify Attacking Creature Step**
**506.1.** The turn player specifies an attacking creature.
* **506.1a** First, the turn player chooses which creature will attack. (This choice is optional.) The chosen creature must be in a valid state to attack. It must be untapped, must have been in the Battle Zone continuously since the beginning of the turn, and must not have its ability to attack negated by any effect. A creature requiring certain conditions to attack cannot be specified as an attacking creature if those conditions are not met.
* **506.1b** If the turn player is under some kind of compulsion (an effect that states the creature attacks, or that the creature attacks if certain conditions are met) for their creature, they must make that creature attack during that turn. The order in which compelled creatures participate in an attack is optional.
* **506.1c** The turn player taps the chosen creature.
* **506.1d** From then on, the chosen creature becomes the attacking creature. It remains the attacking creature until it is removed from "that attack" or until the End of Attack Step.
* **506.1e** The turn player specifies whether the creature attacks the player or a tapped creature.
**506.2.** If no attacking creature was specified, the other sub-steps are skipped.
* **505.2a** If sub-steps are skipped, declarations from those points cannot be made. Effects already on standby can be used.
**506.3.** Next, abilities triggered by the attacking creature being specified trigger, and the effects enter a standby state.
* **506.3a** Abilities that trigger in response to a creature attacking or being attacked only trigger at the point it is designated as the attacking creature. Even if the creature's characteristics change to match the trigger condition of that ability after it has participated in the attack, it will not trigger.
* **506.3b** Some triggered abilities require a declaration of use. When using them, the turn player first declares all the ones they will use. Triggered abilities requiring a declaration of use refer to abilities that trigger in a Private Zone, such as Ninja Strike or Revolution Change. (Reference 603.2e)
* **506.3c** After that, the turn player's standby effects are processed in the order of their choosing.
* **506.3d** The non-turn player makes declarations for their triggered abilities that require a declaration of use. Even if the condition was not met at the point the attacking creature was specified, if the condition is met at this point, the declaration of use is possible.
* **506.3e** After that, the non-turn player's standby effects are processed in the order of their choosing. If a turn player's effect triggers during this process, after resolving the effect currently being processed, the turn player's effect is prioritized, and then it returns to processing the non-turn player's side.

**507. Specify Blocking Creature Step**
**507.1.** The non-turn player chooses a creature to use an ability that changes the attack target.
* **507.1a** When wanting to use an ability that changes the attack target, such as Blocker or Guardman, the non-turn player chooses 1 or more creatures that can use that ability. If choosing multiple, they are chosen simultaneously. The chosen creatures must be in a valid state to use that ability. A creature requiring certain conditions to use an ability that changes the attack target cannot be chosen if those conditions are not met.
  * *Example: You cannot choose a tapped creature with Blocker as the creature to use the ability.*
  * *Example: If an opponent's creature is attacking the player, you cannot choose a creature with Guardman as the creature to use the ability. This applies even if you also have a creature with Blocker.*
  * *Example: If a creature with the "cannot be blocked" ability is attacking, you cannot choose a creature with Blocker.*
  * *Example: In a situation where an untapped creature with Blocker is being attacked, that attacked creature cannot be chosen as the creature to use the Blocker ability.*
* **507.1b** If the non-turn player has an effect that forces a block on their own creature, they must definitely choose that creature as the creature using the Blocker ability. If there is an effect forcing a block on multiple creatures, all of them must be chosen.
  * *Example: In a situation where you have 2 of your own creatures that can use the Blocker ability, if the opponent's 《Komusubi Zaminohana》 attacks, you must choose both of them as the creatures using the ability.*
**507.2.** Resolve the ability of the chosen creature. If multiple creatures using abilities were chosen, the non-turn player resolves those abilities one by one in the order of their choosing, and as a result, the attack target is changed by the ability resolved last.
* **507.2a.** Ultimately, if the attack target is changed to the creature that used the ability due to a Blocker ability, the chosen creature is specified as the blocking creature. It remains the blocking creature until it is removed from "that attack" or until the End of Attack Step.
  * *Example: When using the Blocker abilities of 2 creatures, only the 1 creature whose effect was resolved last becomes the blocking creature.*
* **507.2b.** Ultimately, if the attack target is changed to the creature that used the ability due to a non-Blocker ability (like Guardman), the chosen creature is not a blocking creature.
* **507.2c.** The attacking creature blocked by the blocking creature becomes the "blocked creature." If no blocking creature exists, the creature becomes the "unblocked creature." It remains in that state until removed from "that attack" or until the End of Attack Step. Even if the creature that blocked it is removed from "that attack," the blocked creature remains blocked.
**507.3.** Abilities triggered by the blocking creature being specified trigger, and all effects enter a standby state.
* **507.3a.** A triggered ability with the trigger condition "When this creature blocks" triggers by the creature possessing that ability itself being specified as the blocking creature.
* **507.3b.** An ability that triggers when a creature with specific characteristics blocks will not trigger if that creature does not possess those characteristics at the time it is specified as a blocking creature or blocks via some effect. An ability that triggers when a creature with specific characteristics is blocked will not trigger if the blocked creature does not possess those characteristics at the time it becomes a blocked creature. An ability that triggers when a creature is blocked by a creature with specific characteristics will not trigger if the latter creature does not possess those characteristics at the time it becomes a blocking creature. These abilities will not trigger even if the creature's characteristics change later to meet the condition.
  * *Example: When there is a creature with the ability "Whenever this creature is blocked by a Light creature, destroy that creature at the end of the attack," if this creature is blocked by a Darkness creature, and then that creature later changes to Light, this ability does not trigger.*
* **507.3c.** An ability saying "When a creature attacks and is not blocked" triggers when the blocking creature for that creature is not specified. It does not trigger if a blocking creature is specified and then later removed from "that attack."
* **507.3d.** Some triggered abilities require a declaration of use. When using them, the turn player first declares all the ones they will use. (Reference 603.2e)
* **507.3e.** After that, the turn player's standby effects are processed in the order of their choosing.
* **507.3f.** The non-turn player declares the use of triggered abilities requiring a declaration of use.
* **507.3g.** After that, the non-turn player's standby effects are processed in the order of their choosing. If a turn player's effect triggers during this process, after resolving the effect currently being processed, the turn player's effect is prioritized, and then it returns to processing the non-turn player's side.
**507.4.** If a creature enters the Battle Zone in a blocking state, it is a "blocking creature," but in terms of trigger events and effects, it is not treated as having "blocked."

**508. Battle Step**
**508.1.** At this stage, if a creature is attacking another creature or if the attack target has been changed, a battle occurs.

**509. Direct Attack Step**
**509.1.** If the attack target of the attacking creature has not been changed, and the non-turn player has no shields at this point, the attack becomes a Direct Attack. The player who receives a Direct Attack loses the game. This is a state-based action.
**509.2.** If the non-turn player has shields, determine the number of shields the attacking creature breaks.
* **509.2a** A creature with nothing written in its text breaks 1 shield.
* **509.2b** If a creature possesses an ability to break multiple shields, such as W Breaker, it breaks the number of shields specified by that ability.
* **509.2c** If a creature possesses multiple Breaker abilities simultaneously, the player selects which ability to use. At this time, you can specify the W Breaker on a creature possessing both T Breaker and W Breaker simultaneously to break 2 shields, but you cannot choose to specify neither and only break 1 shield.
**509.3.** Choose the specified number of shields to break. Abilities that trigger before a break, such as "Before breaking," trigger at this point.
  * *Example: "Dragon W Breaker", "Master W Breaker", 《MAX The Johnny》's "Before each break" ability.*
**509.4.** Resolve the effects placed on standby by the triggered "Before breaking" abilities. After all those effects are resolved, if there are other effects on standby, resolve them.
* **509.4a** First, from among newly triggered abilities, process the turn player's standby effects in the order of their choosing.
* **509.4b** Next, process the non-turn player's standby effects in the order of their choosing. If a turn player's effect triggers during this process, after resolving the effect currently being processed, the turn player's effect is prioritized, and then it returns to processing the non-turn player's side.
* **509.4c** Even if the attacking creature leaves the Battle Zone as a result of resolving an effect, that creature still breaks the opponent's shields it has already chosen.
**509.5.** The creature breaks the chosen number of shields simultaneously. Even if the number of broken shields does not reach the number specified by the ability, you cannot choose a newly added shield via some method as an additional shield to break.
* **509.5a** S-Trigger can be declared when a shield is broken and added to the hand. If there are multiple S-Triggers, they are declared simultaneously. You must reveal the declared card to the opponent.
* **509.5b** G-Strike can be declared when a shield is broken and added to the hand. If there are multiple G-Strikes, they are declared simultaneously. You must reveal the declared card to the opponent.
* **509.5c** S-Back can be declared by discarding the card added to the hand when a shield is broken. You must reveal the declared card to the opponent. The card discarded at this time is moved from the shield to the graveyard, but it is treated as a "card discarded from the hand."
* **509.5d** Once declarations are finished, add the broken shields to your hand. At this time, if there is a card with "Emblem of Judgment" among the cards added to the hand, you can discard it to declare Sabaki Z.
* **509.5e** After that, resolve S-Trigger, S-Back, G-Strike, and Sabaki Z in any order you choose.
  * *Example: If the 2 shields broken simultaneously were 《Cyber Brain》 and 《Fairy Re:Life》, you can cast 《Cyber Brain》 via "S-Trigger", draw cards, and then use "G-Strike" to choose 1 opponent's creature and prevent it from attacking. Alternatively, you can use "G-Strike" to choose 1 opponent's creature and prevent it from attacking, and then cast 《Cyber Brain》.*
**509.6.** You cannot choose the same shield 2 times with an ability that breaks multiple shields simultaneously. If a shield is not broken due to some method, that shield remains in the Shield Zone.

**510. End of Attack Step**
**510.1.** All abilities that trigger "at the end of the attack" trigger. The triggered effects are processed in order, starting with the turn player.
**510.2.** Next, "during an attack" and "while blocking" effects expire.

**511. End of Turn Step**
**511.1.** Triggered abilities with the trigger condition "at the end of the turn" trigger. Each trigger and its use are performed in the following sequence:
* **511.1a** All triggered abilities not requiring a declaration of use trigger.
* **511.1b** The turn player declares the use of triggered abilities requiring a declaration of use.
* **511.1c** The turn player processes their standby effects.
* **511.1d** Once the turn player has no more standby effects, the non-turn player declares the use of triggered abilities requiring a declaration of use.
* **511.1e** The non-turn player processes their standby effects in the order of their choosing. If a turn player's effect triggers during this process, after resolving the effect currently being processed, the turn player's effect is prioritized, and then it returns to processing the non-turn player's side.
**511.2.** "During that turn" and "until the end of the turn" effects expire simultaneously.

**512. Moving to the Next Turn**
**512.1.** When moving to the next turn, any cards in a pending state at that point are all placed in the Graveyard. At this time, no abilities are newly triggered.

## 6. Execution of Cards, Abilities, and Effects

**600. General**

**601. Execution of Cards**
**601.1.** "Executing a card" means summoning a creature, casting a spell, generating a Cross Gear, fortifying a shield with a Castle, deploying a Field, etc., in order to make that card affect the game.
* **601.1a** The player declares the execution of the card, including any modifications to the execution conditions. If there are effects that increase or decrease the mana cost, the effects to be applied are determined at this point. If, due to effects that modify execution conditions or increase/decrease mana costs, the card being executed itself needs to move to another zone, that effect cannot be used. Furthermore, if the processing required to apply an effect cannot be performed, or if applying another effect first makes the required processing impossible, that effect cannot be used.
  * *Example: When wanting to apply the G-G-G (Gaga-Gaga-Ga) of 《"Batsu Akushu" Brand》, your hand must be exactly 1 card or less, including the 《"Batsu Akushu" Brand》, at the time you declare the execution of the card.*
  * *Example 2: You cannot apply Fushigibirth in a situation where you have no creatures.*
* **601.1b** At the moment the execution of a card is declared, the card enters a pending state. If the execution of the card is prohibited, the declaration itself cannot be made. Also, if an alternative cost cannot be paid, the declaration cannot be made.
* **601.1c** When executing a card located in the Mana Zone, you can tap that executing card, which is in a pending state, to pay its mana cost and civilization.
* **601.1d** When paying a cost, you first pay the civilization.
* **601.1e** The player applies effects that increase or decrease the mana cost, determining the final mana cost of that card.
* **601.1f** The player pays the mana cost. If there is any processing that must be done upon executing the card, that processing is performed.
* **601.1g** Once the mana cost is paid, that card is immediately executed.
* **601.1h** If an ability instructs a player to choose something, the player specifies what to choose. If an ability instructs you to choose multiple things simultaneously, you cannot choose the exact same thing multiple times. If there is an instruction to choose "for each...", or if the same ability exerts multiple effects, you can choose the same thing multiple times.

**602. Handling Activated Abilities**
**602.1.** An activated ability is an ability where you can gain an effect by performing the action that serves as the activation condition. You cannot gain the effect without performing the condition action. Abilities of this type include Tap abilities, Silent Skill, Dynamo, etc.
**602.2.** The activation of the ability and the effect of the ability itself are separate, independent events. Some activation conditions allow specific situations to be replaced, but even in such cases, only the activation part of the ability is replaced; the effect of the ability itself does not replace anything.
**602.3.** A player cannot begin to activate an ability whose activation is prohibited.
* **602.3a** Abilities that can be used when able to attack (Example: Tap abilities) cannot be used if the creature is in a state where it cannot attack due to summoning sickness or some other effect.

**603. Handling Triggered Abilities**
**603.1.** Triggered abilities have a trigger condition and an effect.
**603.2.** Triggered abilities automatically trigger whenever their trigger condition is met. The triggered effect enters a standby state once and waits for resolution.
* **603.2a** At the beginning of a step, all abilities that trigger "At the beginning" trigger.
* **603.2b** A triggered ability triggers exactly once each time its trigger condition is met. However, if the event contains multiple occurrences, it may trigger multiple times.
  * *Example: When there is a creature with an ability whose trigger condition is "Whenever any creature is destroyed," if multiple creatures are destroyed simultaneously, that ability triggers a number of times equal to the number of destroyed creatures.*
* **603.2c** A trigger event using the phrase "becomes" (になる) triggers only when the indicated event occurs. It does not trigger if it was already in that state originally, nor does it trigger again while continuing to be in that state. Similarly, it does not trigger if a card enters that zone already in that state.
  * *Example: An ability that triggers when a creature "becomes tapped" triggers only when a creature in the Battle Zone goes from an untapped state to a tapped state.*
* **603.2d** An ability triggers only when its trigger event actually occurs. It does not trigger for events that were replaced and therefore did not happen.
  * *Example: An ability that triggers by a creature being placed in the Graveyard does not trigger if the creature was placed in another zone instead of being placed in the Graveyard.*
* **603.2e** Some triggered abilities require a declaration of use. "Ninja Strike," "Revolution Change," "Gate of No Moon," and other triggered abilities that trigger in Private Zones all require a declaration of use. These abilities must be declared even when used from a Public Zone, such as the Graveyard or Mana Zone.
* **603.2f** A declaration of use for a triggered ability means that in a situation where only the owner of the card with that triggered ability can verify its trigger (such as when it is in the hand), that player reveals the card and declares it. The triggered ability only triggers after it has been declared. This is an exception to 603.2.
**603.3.** Once an ability triggers, the effect temporarily enters a standby state, and from among all effects on standby at that moment, they are processed in order starting with the turn player's effects.
* **603.3a** Some triggered abilities state that you use the ability of an icon following it. Those abilities enter a standby state including the contents described in the icon at the stage they triggered.
  * *Example: When a creature with Kizuna Plus attacks and places a card underneath it into the Graveyard, even if the creature with the icon is destroyed before the ability resolves, the two icon abilities chosen at the stage the card was placed in the Graveyard during activation are still in a standby state.*
**603.4.** If a triggered ability has an "if" condition, as long as the condition is met at the time of the ability's resolution, the effect resolves. It does not matter whether the condition was met at the exact moment it triggered.
**603.5.** A trigger condition involving a card moving zones is called a "zone change trigger." Triggers conditioned on movement between zones like the hand, Battle Zone, Mana Zone, Graveyard, or deck are all zone change triggered abilities.
* **603.5a** Abilities "when this enters the Battle Zone" trigger when a creature, Cross Gear, Weapon, or Fortress enters the Battle Zone. Every time one or more cards enter the Battle Zone, the game checks all abilities that trigger when entering the Battle Zone, including the triggered abilities possessed by the card(s) that just entered.
* **603.5b** Continuous effects that add or change card characteristics apply instantly the moment the card is placed in a zone; the card is never considered placed in that zone with its original characteristics. Triggered abilities conditioned on characteristics check the characteristics *after* this continuous effect is applied.
  * *Example: When 《Courtney, Balmy Breeze Fairy》 is in play, if you charge a Nature card into your mana, that card is placed in the Mana Zone as a card possessing all civilizations. At this time, if there is a creature with a Space Charge (Darkness) ability, that ability triggers.*
* **603.5c** Some triggered abilities trigger when a card leaves the Battle Zone. To determine whether these abilities trigger, the game needs to "look at the past state." These trigger based on the existence and state of the cards immediately before the event, rather than immediately after.
* **603.5d** For some abilities that trigger when a card leaves the Battle Zone, the ability also triggers when the card possessing the ability itself leaves the Battle Zone. At this time, the ability can only "look at the past state" for the card possessing that ability itself.
  * *Example: When there are 2 creatures and 1 creature with the ability "When a creature is destroyed, draw a card" in the Battle Zone, if you cast a spell that destroys all creatures, that ability triggers only 1 time for the creature itself.*
**603.6.** An effect may create a delayed triggered ability that does something later.
* **603.6a** A delayed triggered ability is created by the execution of a card, another ability, or as a result of applying a replacement effect. Even if its trigger event had occurred before it was actually created, it will not go back in time to exert its effect.
  * *Example: When you cast a spell with the ability "Whenever a creature taps for the first time this turn, untap it," it only triggers for a creature that taps for the first time *after* the spell is actually cast; it does not trigger for a creature that had already tapped before casting the spell.*
**603.7.** There are abilities with trigger conditions like "Before breaking," which trigger immediately before breaking a shield. These abilities trigger when the shield to be broken is chosen, and resolve their effects before the shield is broken.
* **603.7a** After resolving an effect put on standby by an ability that triggers immediately before breaking, if there are other standby effects, resolve all those effects, and then break the chosen shield.
* **603.7b** When resolving an effect put on standby by an ability that triggers immediately before breaking, a shield to be broken may be newly chosen, causing a "Before breaking" ability to trigger anew. In this case, resolve the effect of the most recently triggered "Before breaking" ability prior to resolving other standby effects or breaking the initially chosen shield. After that, if there are other standby effects, resolve them, then break the newly chosen shield, and finally return to the processing of breaking the initially chosen shield.
  * *Example: You attack with 《Kakumeijin, Magical General of Art》 and attempt to break the opponent's shields, triggering the "Before breaking" ability twice. For the 1st trigger, you cast 《Texas Storm》, place 《Kakumeijin, Magical General of Art》 on top of your deck, bring it out again, and attempt to newly break 1 of the opponent's shields. At this time, a 3rd "Before breaking" ability triggers. In the above case, first resolve the 3rd "Before breaking" ability to cast a spell, then break the opponent's shield chosen with 《Texas Storm》. After that, resolve the 2nd "Before breaking" ability from the initial two triggers, and once that finishes, break the 2 shields chosen by the attack of 《Kakumeijin, Magical General of Art》.*

**604. Handling Static Abilities**
**604.1.** Static abilities do not activate or trigger; rather, they constantly continue to do something.
**604.2.** Static abilities generate continuous effects. Some of these can be replacement effects. These effects persist as long as the card possessing the static ability is in the specified zone and has that ability.
**604.3.** Some static abilities are applied while the card is in the zone from which it can be executed (usually the hand). These include abilities that alter the card's mana cost.
**604.4.** A few static abilities exceptionally generate one-shot effects. If these abilities meet the conditions to generate their effect, they apply that effect before resolving other abilities. Also, if they are generated midway through processing an effect, they interrupt that processing to apply the effect. If the condition to generate the one-shot effect from the static ability is still met after the effect is applied, apply that effect again.
  * *Example: The defeat effect of 《Dokindam X, Legendary Forbidden》, the return-to-hand effect of S-MAX Evolution.*
* **604.4a** When a static ability generates a one-shot effect, if there are continuous effects, apply all continuous effects first, and then apply that one-shot effect.
* **604.4b** If there are multiple one-shot effects generated by static abilities, the processing changes depending on whose player's card is the source. If one player's cards are generating multiple one-shot effects, that player applies the one-shot effects in their preferred order. If multiple players' cards are generating one-shot effects, the turn player's effects are applied first.

**605. Resolution of Cards and Abilities**
**605.1.** When resolving, from among the effects in a standby state, the turn player resolves their own effects in any order they like. The order in which the effects entered standby does not matter.
**605.2.** Resolution is divided into several steps.
* **605.2a** If a triggered ability has a condition, check whether it is met at the time of resolution. If it is not met, do nothing. If it is met, continue resolution.
  * *Example: The Awakening ability of 《Space-Time Brawler, Kill》 always triggers at the beginning of your turn. Even if you do not have a creature with power 6000 or more in the Battle Zone before the start of the turn, if a creature with power 6000 or more comes to exist before the resolution, it is possible to flip it to its higher-cost side.*
* **605.2b** When choosing a card or ability, choose as much as possible within the specified range. If you cannot choose, that effect does nothing. If that card or ability has other effects, the other effects resolve normally. If "all" is stated, it does not mean you are choosing them.
* **605.2c** Cards and abilities resolve in the order they are written. However, this sequence of actions may be altered by replacement effects, and the meaning of earlier instructions may change. In some cases, text written later on a card may modify the meaning of the preceding text.
* **605.2d** When game information is required, that value is determined exactly once when the player performs that processing. When an effect requires specific information from a card, it uses the information of its state at that exact moment. For abilities stating the card does something (like battle), it is actually the card performing the action, not the effect. When continuous effects require card information, they constantly check the card's characteristics (civilization, race, name, cost, power).
  * *Example: When choosing a creature with the ability "While in the Battle Zone, this has all civilizations" for the effect "Destroy 1 creature. If you do, put a creature with the same civilization from your hand," it checks the information at that moment (while it is in the Battle Zone).*
  * *Example: If a Command evolves into a non-Command creature while under the effect "Commands cannot attack," it can then attack.*
* **605.2e** When processing an effect that requires specific information of an element in the Battle Zone, if that element has already left the Battle Zone, it references the information immediately before it left. The same applies if that element was sealed.
  * *Example: In a situation where you have 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 with its Hyper Mode released, if your 《Cheer Space Akane》 attacks and "Revolution Changes" into 《Gol-Fantasista, Super Heavy Dragon》, the creature you can put out from your Mana Zone via the effect of 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 is a creature costing 4 or less.*
  * *Example: During the opponent's turn, in a situation where you have 《Gyogyo, Eye of the Revolution》, if the opponent brings out 《Forbidden Engine VV-8》 and its "When this enters" ability seals the 《Forbidden Engine VV-8》 itself, the cost referenced by 《Gyogyo, Eye of the Revolution》's "When an opponent's creature enters the Battle Zone" ability is 6.*
* **605.2f** As the final step of a spell's resolution, that spell is placed in the Graveyard. An ability disappears as the final step of its resolution.
**605.3.** If the card being resolved is anything other than a spell, it enters the Battle Zone if it is an Element. If the card is a Castle, it is placed in the Shield Zone.
**605.4.** While looking at or revealing cards from the deck due to an effect that executes a card from the deck or puts a card from the deck into play, the cards in the deck being looked at (or revealed) — other than the card being executed (or put into play) — belong to the deck, but they are not affected by other effects.
  * *Example: If you look at the top 5 cards of your deck with the effect of 《Justice, Left God of the Holy Spirit》 and cast 《Energy Re:Light》 from among them, the 5 cards being looked at by the effect are not affected by the effect of 《Energy Re:Light》, so you draw the 6th and 7th cards from the top of the deck.*
  * *Example: If you reveal the top 4 cards of your deck with the "Final Revolution" of 《Dogiragon Nova, Azure Guardian》 and put 《Gaizekial, Demonic Emperor Connection》 from among them into the Battle Zone, the card shielded by the "EX Life" of the entering 《Gaizekial, Demonic Emperor Connection》 is the 5th card located beneath the 4 cards you are looking at.*
* **605.4a** At this time, effects already applied to the cards in the deck you are looking at (or revealing) are not nullified.

**606. Effects**
**606.1.** An "effect" is an event that occurs within the game as a result of executing a card or an ability. Spell abilities, activated abilities, and triggered abilities generate one or more one-shot effects or continuous effects when resolved. Static abilities generate one or more continuous effects. The rule text itself is not an effect.
**606.2.** If an effect demands something impossible to fully realize, perform only the possible part.
  * *Example: If a player has only 1 card in hand, an effect saying "Discard 2 cards from your hand" will make them discard only the card they hold.*
**606.3.** Among continuous effects, there are replacement effects.

**607. One-Shot Effects**
**607.1.** A one-shot effect causes something to happen exactly once and ends immediately. Examples include breaking a shield, destroying a creature, or moving a card between zones.
**607.2.** Some one-shot effects instruct the player to do something, not during the resolution of the spell or ability that generated the one-shot effect, but after the game has advanced (usually at a specific point in time).

**608. Continuous Effects**
**608.1.** A continuous effect alters the characteristics of a card or affects players or game rules for a fixed or indefinite period. Continuous effects are generated by the resolution of a spell ability or ability, or by a card's static ability. If there are multiple continuous effects, they apply simultaneously.
**608.2.** Continuous effects generated by the resolution of spell abilities or abilities:
* **608.2a** A continuous effect generated by the resolution of a spell ability or ability lasts for the period specified by the card or ability that generated it (e.g., "until the end of the turn"). If no period is explicitly stated, it lasts until the end of the game.
* **608.2b** When a spell ability or ability generates a continuous effect involving variables, the values of those variables are fixed only at resolution, and will not change afterward.
**608.3.** Continuous effects generated by a card's static ability:
* **608.3a** A continuous effect generated by a static ability is not "fixed." It constantly applies to everything indicated by its text.
* **608.3b** This effect applies constantly as long as the card generating it is in the corresponding zone.
  * *Example: A card with the static ability "All Light creatures get power +1000" generates an effect that continuously grants power +1000 to each Light creature in the Battle Zone. If a creature becomes Light, it gains this modification; if it ceases to be Light, it loses this modification.*
* **608.3c** A continuous effect that modifies a card's characteristics modifies those characteristics the instant the card enters the corresponding zone. It is not modified before entering the zone, nor does it wait to enter the zone and then get modified. Since this type of effect applies at the moment the card enters the corresponding zone, it is applied before deciding whether abilities that trigger upon entering the zone will trigger.
  * *Example: If there is a creature in the Battle Zone with the static ability "All Light creatures gain Blocker," a Light creature enters the Battle Zone already possessing Blocker. It does not enter the Battle Zone as a creature without Blocker and then gain Blocker afterwards. Conversely, because it does not gain Blocker before entering, it cannot be put into the Battle Zone with cards like 《Heaven's Gate》.*

**609. Replacement Effects**
**609.1.** Some continuous effects are replacement effects. A replacement effect applies continuously as an event occurs. It is not fixed in advance. This type of effect waits for a specific target event and replaces all or part of that event with another event. It functions like a "shield" against what affects the target.
* **609.1a** Effects using the word "instead" (かわりに) are replacement effects. The word "instead" indicates how and into what an event is replaced.
**609.2.** A replacement effect must exist before the appropriate event occurs. It cannot go "back in time" to change something that has already happened.
**609.3.** A replacement effect is applied exactly 1 time per event.
**609.4.** When an event is replaced, it is treated as having never occurred. Instead, the modified event occurs, and any triggered abilities resulting from that will trigger. If the modified event cannot be performed, it cannot be replaced.
**609.5.** When replacing an event that has a replacement effect, if that event does not occur, the replacement effect does nothing.
**609.6.** Some replacement effects replace drawing a card.
* **609.6a** When replacing 1 draw out of an instruction to draw multiple cards, the next card is drawn only after the entire processing of that replacement effect is finished.
* **609.6b** If there is a sequence of effects that says to draw a card and then do something to that card, and the draw part is replaced, resulting in the card not being drawn, the additional part does nothing.
**609.7.** Some replacement effects are not continuous effects. Effects where a spell or ability replaces part or all of its own effect during its own resolution are called self-replacement effects.
**609.8.** If multiple replacement effects attempt to modify a single event simultaneously, determine which effect applies by prioritizing the turn player's effects. If the same player has multiple replacement effects, that player chooses which effect is applied.
* **609.8a** When a spell's effect replaces its own movement, that applies with priority over other replacement effects.
  * *Example: After casting 《Brain Charger》 from the Graveyard via the ability of 《Spell Cyrclica, Element Code Sr》, the "Charger" replacement effect takes priority, and 《Brain Charger》 is placed in the Mana Zone.*
  * *Example: In a situation where 《Brocken Voorn》 is in play, after casting 《Life and Earth and Roaring Decisiveness》 from the Mana Zone, the replacement effect "instead of putting it into the graveyard" in the spell ability takes priority, and 《Life and Earth and Roaring Decisiveness》 is placed at the bottom of the deck.*
**609.9.** Multiple events may occur simultaneously, with different replacement effects attempting to modify each of them. In that case, each player first decides which event they will apply their replacement effects to. Once replacement effects are decided for all events, they are applied simultaneously.
* **609.9a** First, starting with the turn player, decide which replacement effect to apply to which event. If the chosen replacement effect requires processing such as moving a card or changing its orientation, the card affected by that processing must also be chosen at this time. Once the turn player finishes deciding the application of their replacement effects, the non-turn player then decides the application of their replacement effects using the same procedure.
* **609.9b** When choosing a card affected by the processing of a replacement effect, you cannot re-choose a card that you already chose when deciding the application of another replacement effect.

## 7. Other Rules

**700. General**
**700.1.** Everything that happens in the game is an event. Multiple events may occur during the execution of a card or the resolution of an ability. The text of a triggered ability or replacement effect defines the event it is looking for. An occurrence that is considered a single event by one ability may be considered multiple events by another ability.
**700.2.** An ability that presents multiple options is said to have "choices."
* **700.2a** For an ability with choices, you select the choice(s) when resolving that ability.
* **700.2b** There are abilities that force a player other than the ability's owner to make a choice. In that case, that player makes the choice at the time the ability resolves.
* **700.2c** There are cards and abilities that force a player other than the card or ability's owner to make a choice. In that case, that player makes the choice at the standard timing when the card or ability would normally make its choice.
**700.3.** If a creature cannot be destroyed or cannot leave the Battle Zone due to rules or effects, it will not be destroyed or leave the Battle Zone. That type of creature will not be destroyed or leave the Battle Zone even if its power becomes 0 or less, and it ignores the state-based action of having its power drop to 0 or less.
**700.4.** If an attacking creature is not blocked, there is no creature that is legally blocking it.
**700.5.** A creature with an ability that says "wins battles" will win the battle regardless of the battling opponent's power.
* **700.5a** If two creatures that both have an ability that says "wins battles" battle each other, both creatures win the battle, and there is no creature that lost that battle.

**701. Keyword Actions**
**701.1.** Most of the processing written in a card's rule text is written in standard language, but some verbs require specific definition. Those "keywords" are game terms, and their meanings are sometimes summarized in reminder text.
**701.2. Tap and Untap**
* **701.2a** To tap a card means to rotate it 90 degrees from its upright position.
* **701.2b** To untap a card means to return it to its upright position.
**701.3. Summon**
* **701.3a** To summon means to put a creature from the zone it is currently in (usually the hand) into the Battle Zone by paying its cost. A player can normally only summon a creature by paying its mana cost during their Main Step.
**701.4. Cast**
* **701.4a** To cast means to execute a spell from the zone it is currently in (usually the hand) by paying its cost, so that the spell's effects occur. A player can normally only cast a spell by paying its mana cost during their Main Step.
**701.5. Execute**
* **701.5a** To execute a card means to pay the cost to put the card into a state where it can perform its effects. Summoning a creature, casting a spell, generating a Cross Gear, and executing a Tamaseed fall under this category.
**701.6. Destroy**
* **701.6a** To destroy a card means to attempt to put it from the Battle Zone into the Graveyard.
* **701.6b** A destroyed card is put into the Graveyard as a result.
**701.7. Discard**
* **701.7a** To discard a card means to put it from the hand into that player's Graveyard. (When using Strike Back, it happens before adding the card to your hand, but it is treated as the same action as discarding.)
* **701.7b** Normally, an effect that forces a player to discard a card allows the affected player to choose the card to be discarded. Some effects may demand discarding at random, or demand that another player chooses it.
**701.8. Charge**
* **701.8a** To charge mana means to put a card from the hand into that player's Mana Zone upside down.
**701.9. Reveal**
* **701.9a** To reveal a card means to show that card to all players for a brief period. If a card is revealed to all players by an effect, it remains revealed until the portion of the effect concerning that card completely finishes. If a card is revealed to all players as a condition for executing it, that card remains revealed until the card resolves.
* **701.9b** Revealing a card does not change the zone where that card exists.
**701.10. Search**
* **701.10a** To search for a card from a certain zone means to look at all the cards in that zone (even if it's a Private Zone) and find a card that meets the conditions.
* **701.10b** If it is necessary to search a Private Zone for a card that meets certain conditions, such as a specific card type or civilization, you do not have to find all of them even if they exist.
* **701.10c** Unless the effect involving the search states that you must reveal the found card(s) to all players, there is no need to reveal those cards to all players.
**701.11. Shuffle**
* **701.11a** To shuffle the deck or a pile of face-down cards means to randomize the cards within it so that no player knows their order.
* **701.11b** There are effects that have a player search the deck for a card, shuffle, and then place the searched card in a specific location in the deck. In that case, those cards are not treated as having left the deck, but they are not included in the shuffle. Also, all cards in the deck excluding those cards are shuffled.
**701.12. Block**
* **701.12a** To block means to intercept an attacking creature's attack. Only a creature with the Blocker ability can block.
**701.13. Battle**
* **701.13a** To battle means to compare the power between creatures to determine a winner and a loser. The creature with the higher power wins the battle; if equal, or if the power is lower, the creature loses the battle.
* **701.13b** A creature that loses a battle is destroyed.
**701.14. Break**
* **701.14a** To break a shield means to add the shield to its owner's hand. This occurs via a creature's attack or other effects.
* **701.14b** An effect that adds (returns) a shield to the hand behaves the same as a break, but effects conditioned on "breaking" are not applied.
  * *Example: If you attack an opponent's creature with 《Kizugui Henka》 and add your own shield to your hand via its "When this attacks" ability, it does not count as having broken a shield. Therefore, you cannot use Kirifudash at the end of that attack.*
* **701.14c** When multiple shields are broken by an effect, they move to the hand simultaneously. This behaves differently from breaking via Breaker abilities.
* **701.14d** An effect that breaks additional shields affects both breaks from attacks and breaks from effects.
**701.15. Evolve**
* **701.15a** To evolve means to place an Evolution Creature on top of a card, or to place an Evolution Cross Gear on top of a card.
**701.16. Generate**
* **701.16a** To generate means to put a Cross Gear from the zone it is currently in (usually the hand) into the Battle Zone by paying its cost. A player can normally only generate a Cross Gear by paying its mana cost during their Main Step.
**701.17. Cross**
* **701.17a** To cross means to place a Cross Gear under a creature so that the card portion remains visible.
**701.18. Link**
* **701.18a** To link means to connect 2 or more cards side-by-side. Gods, Psychic Super Creatures, certain Dragheart Creatures, and Zerom cards can link.
* **701.18b** A creature linked from 2 or more cards treats the entire linked set of cards as 1 creature.
**701.19. Fortify**
* **701.19a** To fortify means to put a Castle from the zone it is currently in (usually the hand) onto a specified shield by paying its cost. A player can normally only fortify a shield with a Castle by paying its mana cost during their Main Step.
**701.20. Awaken**
* **701.20a** To awaken means to flip a double-sided Psychic Creature card so that the other side becomes visible.
**701.21. Gachinko Judge**
* **701.21a** To Gachinko Judge means each player reveals the top 1 card of their deck and then puts it at the bottom of their deck.
* **701.21b** The player who used the Gachinko Judge ability wins the Gachinko Judge if they reveal a card with a mana cost greater than or equal to the opponent's.
* **701.21c** If a card with 2 costs like a Twinpact is revealed due to a Gachinko Judge ability, the player who revealed it decides which cost to reference.
**701.22. Dragsolution (Ryukai)**
* **701.22a** To Dragsolve means to flip a double-sided Dragheart card so that the other side becomes visible.
**701.23. Remove (a Seal)**
* **701.23a** To remove a seal means to move a seal from the Battle Zone to another zone.
* **701.23b** When removing a seal due to the state-based action of a Command entering the Battle Zone, that seal is placed in the Graveyard.
**701.24. Attach (a Seal)**
* **701.24a** To attach a seal means to place the top card of the deck face down on top of a card in the Battle Zone.
**701.25. Forbidden Release (Kindan Kaihou)**
* **701.25a** To undergo Forbidden Release means to flip the double-sided Forbidden Heartbeat card so that the other side becomes visible.
**701.26. Swap / Switch**
* **701.26a** To swap means to exchange the locations of cards in 2 zones. If a creature or Tamaseed is put into the Battle Zone by a swapping effect, the swapped-in creature or Tamaseed inherits the orientation and state of the creature or Tamaseed before the swap (Creatures swapped via Revolution Change or J-Change are in an attacking state).
* **701.26b** When resolving an effect that swaps an element in the Battle Zone, if the card movement does not occur normally due to a replacement effect, etc., the swap does not take place. Neither card moves zones, and their states do not change at all.
  * *Example: When attacking with 《Bakunetsu DX Batrai Bushin》, if you use the Revolution Change of 《Dogiragon Ken, Azure Captain》, because the card does not leave the Battle Zone due to Dragon Evasion (Ryukaihi), the swap does not occur. As a result, 《Bakunetsu DX Batrai Bushin》 continues the attack, and 《Dogiragon Ken, Azure Captain》 remains in the hand.*
  * *Example: When attacking with 《True Heavenly Emperor Never End》, if you use Revolution Change and use True Escape upon swapping, the swap does not occur. As a result, 《True Heavenly Emperor Never End》 does not move from the Battle Zone, and True Escape is not resolved.*
* **701.26c** There are effects that entirely swap the cards of 2 zones. These resolve even if one of the zones has 0 cards. Also, even if the movement of some cards does not occur normally due to replacement effects, etc., the swap of the remaining cards is still performed.
  * *Example: 《Descent of the Demon Heavens》, 《All Sunrise》*
**701.27. Deploy**
* **701.27a** To deploy means to put a Field from the zone it is currently in (usually the hand) into the Battle Zone by paying its cost. A player can normally only deploy a Field by paying its mana cost during their Main Step.
**701.28. Turn Upside Down**
* **701.28a** To turn upside down means to orient the Field upside down relative to its upright position.
**701.29. Forbidden Explosion (Kindan Bakuhatsu)**
* **701.29a** To undergo Forbidden Explosion means to flip the Final Forbidden Field, composed of 5 double-sided cards, so that the other side is visible, reassembling the 5 cards into 1 Final Forbidden Creature.
**701.30. GR Summon**
* **701.30a** To GR Summon means to treat the top 1 card of your Ultra GR as having its cost paid, summon it, and put it into the Battle Zone.
**701.31. Zerom Birth (Zeiron Baitan)**
* **701.31a** To undergo Zerom Birth means to flip the double-sided Ritual of Zerom and the 4 Nebulas of Zerom so that the other side is visible, reassembling the 5 cards into 1 Zerom Creature.
**701.32. Turn into a Shield (Shieldify)**
* **701.32a** To turn a card into a shield means to place it face down in the Shield Zone as a new shield.
* **701.32b** To turn a card into a face-up shield means to place it face up in the Shield Zone as a new shield.
**701.33. Send to the Abyss**
* **701.33a** To send a card to the abyss means to put it into the Abyss Zone.
* **701.33b** Moving a card sent to the abyss to the Abyss Zone cannot be prevented by effects or rules.
  * *Example: Replacement effects applied instead of leaving, such as "EX Life," cannot be applied to a card sent to the abyss.*
  * *Example: Even if a card sent to the abyss was under the influence of a continuous effect that prevents it from leaving, such as 《Duel of Flashes》, it still moves to the Abyss Zone.*
  * *Example: If linked Gods are sent to the abyss, all component cards move to the Abyss Zone, not just 1 of them.*

**702. Turn-based Actions**
**702.1.** Turn-based actions are game processes that occur automatically according to the progression of the turn. Other effects cannot be processed before turn-based actions.
* **702.1a** Abilities that wait for a specific step to begin are triggered abilities, not turn-based actions.
**702.2.** When a step begins, if turn-based actions exist in that step, those turn-based actions are processed first. This occurs before checking for state-based actions and before triggered abilities enter a standby state.
**702.3.** Turn-based actions are as follows:
* **702.3a** The turn player untaps their cards in the Battle Zone and their cards in the Mana Zone simultaneously. After that, resolve all triggered abilities for the beginning of the turn.
* **702.3b** Immediately after the Draw Step begins, the turn player draws 1 card.
* **702.3c** Immediately after the Specify Attacking Creature Step begins, the turn player specifies an attacking creature.
* **702.3d** Immediately after the Specify Blocking Creature Step begins, the non-turn player specifies a blocking creature.

**703. State-based Actions**
**703.1.** State-based actions are game processes that occur automatically depending on the game state. Other effects cannot be processed before state-based actions.
* **703.1a** Abilities that wait for specific conditions are triggered abilities, not state-based actions.
* **703.1b** State-based actions are determined to occur taking into account the processing of static abilities and continuous effects.
**703.2.** State-based actions are processes checked by the game, and do not belong to any player.
**703.3.** The game constantly checks whether any of the conditions for state-based actions to occur are met, and if any apply, it processes all those state-based actions simultaneously as a single event. If state-based actions occur as a result of a check, the check is repeated again after the processing.
**703.4.** State-based actions include the following:
* **703.4a** A player who received a Direct Attack loses the game.
* **703.4b** A player whose deck has reached 0 cards loses the game.
* **703.4c** A creature with power 0 or less is destroyed.
* **703.4d** A creature that lost a battle is destroyed as a result of the battle.
* **703.4e** If the information of linked Gods becomes invalid, the link detaches.
* **703.4f** If Gods leave the Battle Zone, only 1 of them leaves the Battle Zone.
* **703.4g** A standalone Cell in the Battle Zone is placed in the Graveyard.
* **703.4h** When only the card on top of an Evolution Creature leaves the Battle Zone, the cards underneath are reconstructed.
* **703.4i** A face-up card in the Battle Zone that does not have a valid type is placed in the Graveyard.
* **703.4j** When a Command enters the Battle Zone, its owner places one seal into the Graveyard from among the cards with seals attached that share the same civilization as that Command.
* **703.4k** When a fortified shield leaves the Shield Zone, the Castle is placed in the owner's Graveyard. If using the "S-Trigger" ability of that shield, it is placed in the Graveyard immediately after use.
* **703.4l** When another D2 Field enters the Battle Zone, the D2 Field that was previously in the Battle Zone is placed in its owner's Graveyard.
* **703.4m** A standalone Weapon in the Battle Zone is placed in the Graveyard.

## 8. Special Cards

**800. General**
**800.1.** There are cards in the game that require special processing. These creatures include Evolution Creatures, NEO Creatures, Gods, Psychic Creatures, Draghearts, etc.

**801. Evolution Creatures**
**801.1.** Evolution Creatures are creatures that can be put into the Battle Zone by stacking them on top of a base card used for evolution.
* **801.1a** If there is no valid evolution base, you cannot put an Evolution Creature into the Battle Zone.
* **801.1b** When putting an Evolution Creature into the Battle Zone using multiple cards as the evolution base, stack the evolution base cards in whatever order you like beneath the Evolution Creature.
**801.2.** Even if a card in the Battle Zone evolves into an Evolution Creature, it is treated as the same creature as the card before evolution. A tapped card remains tapped when it evolves, and effects applied to that card continue to apply even after it evolves.
  * *Example: An Evolution Creature evolved from a creature that received a power -2000 modification until the end of the turn will also receive the power -2000 modification. Similarly, a GV Evolution Creature evolved using 3 base creatures that each received a power -2000 modification will receive a power -6000 modification.*
* **801.2a** If an attacking creature evolves, that attack is continued.
* **801.2b** If a creature that evolves from 2 or more cards uses both tapped and untapped cards as its evolution bases, the Evolution Creature is put into the Battle Zone in a tapped state.
**801.3.** Evolution Creatures do not suffer from summoning sickness.
**801.4.** When only the topmost card of an Evolution Creature leaves the Battle Zone, the cards underneath remain in the Battle Zone in a valid state. This is called Reconstruction.
* **801.4a** During Reconstruction, if the card immediately below the Evolution Creature that left the Battle Zone is a card that can exist standalone in the Battle Zone, it remains in the Battle Zone as is. If it is anything else, it is placed in the Graveyard.
* **801.4b** During Reconstruction, if there are multiple cards under the Evolution Creature that left the Battle Zone, keep the topmost card that can exist standalone in the Battle Zone in the Battle Zone, and leave the others stacked beneath that card. If the topmost card is a card that cannot exist in the Battle Zone, it is placed in the Graveyard. Repeat this process until the topmost card becomes a card that can exist standalone in the Battle Zone, and that newly top card remains in the Battle Zone.
* **801.4c** The reconstructed card does not newly enter the Battle Zone; it is treated as having already been in the Battle Zone. Abilities that trigger when entering the Battle Zone do not trigger. Also, any effects it was receiving before Reconstruction are inherited after Reconstruction. Furthermore, if it is a creature and had existed under the Evolution Creature since the beginning of the turn, it does not newly suffer from summoning sickness.
* **801.4d** When a creature or Tamaseed remains due to Reconstruction, its tap/untap orientation is the same as the Evolution Creature that left the Battle Zone. If a card that is neither a creature nor a Tamaseed, such as a Field, remains, that card becomes in its correct upright position, regardless of the orientation of the Evolution Creature that left.
  * *Example: If an untapped Star Evolution Creature leaves and Reconstruction occurs, and the underlying D2 Field remains in the Battle Zone, that D2 Field remains in a horizontal orientation.*

**802. NEO Creatures**
**802.1.** A NEO Creature is a creature that can be treated as a normal creature, and can also be put into the Battle Zone as a NEO Evolution Creature by using its NEO Evolution ability.
**802.2.** A NEO Creature is treated as a "NEO Evolution Creature" while it is in the Battle Zone with a card underneath it via the NEO Evolution ability, or while it is attempting to enter the Battle Zone as a NEO Evolution Creature. While in other zones, it is treated as a non-evolution creature.
  * *Example: When revealing the top 6 cards of the deck with 《Evolution Blueprint》, even if a NEO Creature is included, it cannot be added to the hand because it is treated as a normal creature.*
  * *Example: When attempting to put a NEO Creature into the Battle Zone from the Graveyard as a NEO Evolution Creature via an effect, it enters the Battle Zone tapped due to the ability of 《To That Child, with Pity》.*
**802.3.** If a NEO Creature has a card underneath it, it is treated as a "NEO Evolution Creature" and does not suffer from summoning sickness. However, if the underlying card is removed by some method during the same turn it was played, it is no longer a "NEO Evolution Creature" and cannot attack due to "summoning sickness."
**802.4.** Even if you put it into the Battle Zone without using the NEO Evolution ability, if a card gets placed underneath it by some method, it is treated as a "NEO Evolution Creature."

**803. G-NEO Creatures**
**803.1.** Some NEO Creatures are also G-NEO Creatures.
**803.2.** For a G-NEO Creature, when it leaves the Battle Zone while it has a card placed underneath it and is treated as a G-NEO Evolution Creature, all the cards placed under the G-NEO Creature leave instead.
* **803.2a** The "when leaving" effect of a G-NEO Creature is a replacement effect. When leaving the Battle Zone, if another replacement effect was applied first, or if it leaves the Battle Zone due to another replacement effect, the "when leaving" effect cannot be applied.

**804. Gods**
**804.1.** A God is a creature capable of linking with other specific Gods.
* **804.1a** Gods link upon entering the Battle Zone. They do not link after entering the Battle Zone; they enter the Battle Zone in a linked state. This is a state-defining effect.
* **804.1b** A linked God is treated as 1 creature possessing all the names, abilities, civilizations, and other characteristics of each God constituting it. However, note that it does not alter the individual characteristics of each component card; it is treated as 1 creature collectively.
* **804.1c** When a God enters the Battle Zone and triggers a card ability, even if that God links to become 1 creature, the ability references the characteristics of the card that actually entered the Battle Zone.
* **804.1d** When a God enters the Battle Zone, if a replacement effect or a state-defining effect other than G-Link is applied, even if that God links to become 1 creature, it references the characteristics of the card actually entering the Battle Zone.
**804.2.** To link Gods, a valid G-Link ability is required.
* **804.2a** G-Link, which specifies names and positions, references the characteristics of the individual cards.
  * *Example: Linked 《Absolute God Zen》 and 《Ultimate God Aku》 possess the names of each respective God as 1 God entity. However, this does not mean the individual card of 《Absolute God Zen》 has gained the characteristic of 《Ultimate God Aku》. Therefore, you cannot link another 《Absolute God Zen》 to the right of the linked 《Absolute God Zen》.*
* **804.2b** The G-Link ability constantly checks whether the link is valid. If it enters an invalid state, the link is immediately detached.
* **804.2c** When linking Gods with the Center G-Link 360 ability, there are cases where adjacent positions might not actually be directly linked. For example, when linking A-B-C-D in a U-shape, A and D are adjacent but not directly linked. When connecting a God to an unlinked position, it is limited to the range that can be formed on a 2D plane. You cannot newly link a God into a position that is physically blocked.
**804.3.** Even if a God newly links, it is treated as the same creature that was already in the Battle Zone. A tapped God remains tapped even if it links, and effects applied to that creature continue to apply after it links.
  * *Example: If a God that received a power -2000 modification until the end of the turn links, the power -2000 modification remains even after linking.*
* **804.3a** If the right and left sides of a God capable of a 3-piece link have different orientations (tapped and untapped), and you play the center God to link all 3, the resulting state is tapped.
* **804.3b** If an attacking God links, that attack is continued.
**804.4.** Even if a God-linked creature becomes unlinked and becomes an unlinked God, that creature is treated as having been the same creature. Effects applied to that creature continue to apply even after the link is detached.
* **804.4a** When a God composed of 3 or more cards leaves the Battle Zone and becomes 2 or more Gods, each God inherits all effects that were applied to it. If it was attacking, the attacking player chooses one of them and continues the attack.
**804.5.** Linked Gods do not suffer from summoning sickness.
* **804.5a** Once linked, if a God's link is detached, as long as it has been in the Battle Zone since the beginning of the turn, it will not newly suffer from summoning sickness.
**804.6.** When a player specifies 1 card name due to an effect, that player can specify the card name of any 1 card included in the linked God, but cannot specify all of them.
**804.7.** When a God leaves the Battle Zone, only 1 of those cards leaves the Battle Zone. This is a state-based action, not a replacement effect.

**805. Psychic Creatures**
**805.1.** A Psychic Creature is a card that, unlike normal cards which have a Duel Masters card front and a card back, has a Duel Masters card front on both sides. Each face may possess the ability to change from one face to the other.
* **805.1a** Many Psychic Creatures have an Awakening ability to "flip" the creature when specific conditions are met.
* **805.1b** Some Psychic Creatures have a Release ability that flips them to the lower-cost face instead of leaving the Battle Zone. This is a replacement effect.
* **805.1c** Some Psychic Creatures have an Awakening Link ability that simultaneously flips multiple Psychic Creatures and links them into a Psychic Super Creature.
* **805.1d** Each player can look at both faces of a Psychic Creature regardless of which zone it is in.
**805.2.** Each face of a Psychic Creature possesses its own independent set of characteristics.
**805.3.** When spells, abilities, effects, or rules require information about a Psychic Creature, they reference only the information provided by the face that is currently face-up at the time of application.
**805.4.** Psychic Creatures are placed in the Hyperspatial Zone at the start of the game.
* **805.4a** Psychic Creatures cannot be summoned by paying their mana cost.
* **805.4b** When a Psychic Creature is placed in any zone other than the Battle Zone, it immediately returns to the Hyperspatial Zone.
**805.5.** Even if a Psychic Creature is flipped, it is treated as the same creature. A tapped Psychic Creature remains tapped even if it awakens, and effects applied to that creature continue to apply even after it flips.
  * *Example: If a Psychic Creature that received a power -2000 modification until the end of the turn flips, the power -2000 modification remains even after it flips.*
  * *Example: If an attacking Psychic Creature flips, that attack is continued.*
**805.6.** Awakened Psychic Creatures do not suffer from summoning sickness.
* **805.6a** Even if an awakened Psychic Creature flips back due to an effect other than Awakening, as long as it has been in the Battle Zone since the beginning of the turn, it will not suffer from summoning sickness.
**805.7.** When a player specifies 1 card name due to an effect, that player can specify the card name of either face of the Psychic Creature, but cannot specify both.

**806. Psychic Super Creatures**
**806.1.** A Psychic Super Creature is a creature composed of multiple Psychic Cells, and is also treated as a Psychic Creature.
* **806.1a** Psychic Cells cannot exist standalone in the Battle Zone. If a Psychic Cell somehow ends up in the Battle Zone, it is placed in the Graveyard by a state-based action, and then immediately returns to the Hyperspatial Zone.
* **806.1b** Some Psychic Super Creatures have a Link Release ability that, when leaving the Battle Zone, returns one of the composing Psychic Cells to the Hyperspatial Zone and flips the remaining cards. This is not a replacement effect.
* **806.1c** The Link Release ability applies not to the Psychic Super Creature itself, but when the composing Psychic Cells leave the Battle Zone.
* **806.1d** A Psychic Cell by itself has no cost, and when referenced, its cost is treated as 0.
* **806.1e** When a Psychic Cell leaves the Battle Zone, it is not treated as a creature leaving.
* **806.1f** Each Psychic Cell possesses the civilizations possessed by the Psychic Super Creature.
**806.2.** Even if a Psychic Super Creature Link Releases and becomes multiple Psychic Creatures, those creatures are treated as having been the same creature. If a tapped Psychic Super Creature Link Releases, the resulting separated Psychic Creatures remain tapped, and the effects applied to that creature are inherited by each of the separated Psychic Creatures.
* **806.2a** When a Psychic Super Creature composed of 3 or more cards leaves the Battle Zone and becomes 2 or more creatures, each creature inherits all effects that were applied. If it was attacking, the attacking player chooses one of them and continues the attack.

**807. Draghearts**
**807.1.** A Dragheart is a card that, unlike normal cards which have a Duel Masters card front and a card back, has a Duel Masters card front on both sides. The other face of a Dragheart Creature is either a Dragheart Weapon, a Dragheart Fortress, or both. Each face may possess the ability to change from one face to the other.
* **807.1a** A Dragheart Creature can Dragsolve (Ryukai) from a Dragheart Weapon or Dragheart Fortress to become face-up. At this time, its orientation does not change.
* **807.1b** Some Dragheart Creatures have a Dragon Evasion (Ryukaihi) ability that flips them to the lower-cost face instead of leaving the Battle Zone. This is a replacement effect.
* **807.1c** Each player can look at both faces of a Dragheart regardless of which zone it is in.
**807.2.** Each face of a Dragheart possesses its own independent set of characteristics.
**807.3.** When spells, abilities, effects, or rules require information about a Dragheart, they reference only the information provided by the face that is currently face-up at the time of application.
**807.4.** Draghearts are placed in the Hyperspatial Zone at the start of the game.
* **807.4a** Draghearts cannot be executed by paying their mana cost.
* **807.4b** When a Dragheart is placed in any zone other than the Battle Zone, it immediately returns to the Hyperspatial Zone.
**807.5.** Dragheart Creatures suffer from summoning sickness.
* **807.5a** A Dragheart Creature cannot attack unless it has been in the Battle Zone since the beginning of the turn. However, it does not matter which face is up at the beginning of the turn. If it exists in the Battle Zone as a Dragheart Weapon at the beginning of the turn and then Dragsolves, that Dragheart Creature is treated as having been in the Battle Zone since the beginning of the turn.
**807.6.** When a player specifies 1 card name due to an effect, that player can specify the card name of either face of the Dragheart, but cannot specify both.

**808. Dragheart Super Creatures**
**808.1.** A Dragheart Super Creature is a creature composed of multiple Dragheart Cells, and is also treated as a Dragheart Creature.
* **808.1a** Dragheart Super Creatures do not suffer from summoning sickness.
* **808.1b** Some Dragheart Super Creatures have a Dragon Soul Evasion (Ryukonkaihi) ability that, when leaving the Battle Zone, chooses 1 of its Dragheart Cells to return to the Hyperspatial Zone instead, and flips the remaining cards. This is a replacement effect.
* **808.1c** A Dragheart Cell by itself has no cost or race, and when referenced, its cost is treated as 0.
* **808.1d** When a Dragheart Cell leaves the Battle Zone, it is not treated as a creature leaving. However, replacement effects applied when the creature leaves are also applied when a Dragheart Cell leaves the Battle Zone.
* **808.1e** Each Dragheart Cell possesses the civilizations possessed by the Dragheart Super Creature.

**809. Forbidden Heartbeats**
**809.1.** A Forbidden Heartbeat is a card that, unlike normal cards which have a back, has a Duel Masters card front on both sides. The other face of a Forbidden Heartbeat is a Forbidden Creature. A Forbidden Heartbeat possesses the ability to change into the Forbidden Creature face.
* **809.1a** A Forbidden Heartbeat can undergo Forbidden Release to flip over.
* **809.1b** Each player can look at both faces of a Forbidden Heartbeat regardless of which zone it is in.
**809.2.** When spells, abilities, effects, or rules require Forbidden information, if it is in the Battle Zone, they reference only the information provided by the face that is currently face-up at the time of application. If it is in a zone other than the Battle Zone, either side can be treated as the front.
**809.3.** Forbidden Heartbeats are placed in the specified zone at the start of the game.
**809.4.** When a player specifies 1 card name due to an effect, that player can specify the card name of either face of the Forbidden, but cannot specify both.

**810. Twinpacts**
**810.1.** A Twinpact card is a new type of card that encompasses 2 card types within 1 card. They have 2 different costs and card types, but when referencing their characteristics as a card, the correct information must always be referenced.
**810.2.** Twinpact is a characteristic a card possesses.
**810.3.** When referenced as a card, the owner of the Twinpact card can choose whichever valid card type they prefer.
* **810.3a** When referencing a specific card type, reference only the information of that part.
**810.4.** When referencing the card name of a Twinpact card, it is treated as a card with a name combining the two parts. When referencing the card name of a Twinpact card existing as a creature in the Battle Zone, reference only the creature side.

**811. GR Creatures**
**811.1.** A GR Creature is a card that has a Duel Masters card front on one side and a unique backing specific to GR Creature cards on the other.
**811.2.** GR Creatures are placed in the Ultra GR Zone at the start of the game.
* **811.2a** GR Creatures cannot be summoned by paying their mana cost. They can be put into the Battle Zone via the abilities of Spells, Creatures, and Auras.
* **811.2b** When a GR Creature is placed in any zone other than the Battle Zone, it immediately returns to the Ultra GR Zone.

**812. Ritual of Zerom, Nebula of Zerom**
**812.1.** Ritual of Zerom and Nebula of Zerom are cards that, instead of having a card back, have a Duel Masters card front on both sides. One face is Ritual of Zerom/Nebula of Zerom, and the other face is a component card of the Zerom Creature. Ritual of Zerom possesses the ability to change the Ritual and Nebulas of Zerom into the Zerom Creature face.
* **812.1a** Ritual of Zerom and Nebula of Zerom can undergo Zerom Birth to flip over.
* **812.1b** Each player can look at both faces of Ritual of Zerom and Nebula of Zerom regardless of which zone they are in.
**812.2.** When spells, abilities, effects, or rules require information about Zerom, if it is in the Battle Zone, they reference only the information provided by the face that is currently face-up at the time of application. If it is in a zone other than the Battle Zone, either side can be treated as the front.
**812.3.** Ritual of Zerom and Nebula of Zerom are placed in the specified zone at the start of the game.
**812.4.** When a player specifies 1 card name due to an effect, that player can specify the card name of either face of Zerom, but cannot specify both.
* **812.4a** Ritual of Zerom possesses a card name, but Nebula of Zerom does not possess a card name.

**813. Star Evolution Creatures**
**813.1.** A Star Evolution Creature is an Evolution Creature where, when leaving the Battle Zone, only the topmost card leaves instead.
* **813.1a** The "when leaving" effect of a Star Evolution Creature is a replacement effect. When leaving the Battle Zone, if another replacement effect was applied first, or if it leaves the Battle Zone due to another replacement effect, the "when leaving" effect cannot be applied.
* **813.1b** Forbidden Star Evolution Creatures are Star Evolution Creatures that can be put into the Battle Zone without requiring an evolution base. When leaving the Battle Zone, similar to Star Evolution Creatures, only the topmost card leaves instead.

**814. King Cells**
**814.1.** King Cells are cards that cannot be executed standalone. They can only be executed as a combined card when King Cells with specified card names are in the hand or Mana Zone.
* **814.1a** A King Cell by itself has no cost or race, and when referenced, its cost is treated as 0.
* **814.1b** King Cells possess the civilizations of the colors of their respective mana symbols. Even while combined, when the card itself is referenced, it is treated as possessing only those civilizations.
* **814.1c** When executing King Cells as a combined card, you must pay the mana cost indicated in the top left of the combined state. At this time, if part of the King Cells are in the Mana Zone, you can tap those cards, which are in a pending state, to pay the mana cost and civilization. The King Cells combine after the cost is paid.
  * *Example: When combining and summoning 《Volzeos-Balamord》, you must pay 9 mana including 5 civilizations of mana.*
  * *Example: When combining 《Authority of the New World King》 in your hand with 《Thought of the New World King》 and 《Fighting Spirit of the New World King》 placed in your Mana Zone to summon 《Volzeos-Balamord》, you can pay the mana cost from the 《Thought of the New World King》 and 《Fighting Spirit of the New World King》 in your Mana Zone.*
* **814.1d** If a portion of the King Cells constituting the creature leaves the Battle Zone, the remaining King Cells cannot exist in the Battle Zone and are immediately placed in the Graveyard.
* **814.1e** When a King Cell leaves the Battle Zone, it is not treated as a creature leaving. However, replacement effects applied when the creature leaves are also applied when a King Cell leaves the Battle Zone.

**815. S-MAX Evolution Creatures**
**815.1.** An S-MAX Evolution Creature is an Evolution Creature that can be put into the Battle Zone without requiring an evolution base.
* **815.1a** Only 1 S-MAX Evolution Creature can exist in the Battle Zone per player. If there are 2 or more S-MAX Evolution Creatures belonging to 1 player in the Battle Zone, leave 1 of them and return all others to the hand. This is a static ability.

**816. Hyper Mode**
**816.1.** Some creatures possess Hyper Mode. A creature with Hyper Mode has Hyper Mode abilities and power listed in addition to its normal abilities and power.
* **816.1a** Usually, the normal abilities are written in the upper part of the text box, and the Hyper Mode abilities are written in the lower part.
* **816.1b** Usually, the normal power is written in the bottom left of the card, and the Hyper Mode power is written on the right.
**816.2.** While Hyper Mode is released, the Hyper Mode abilities and power become active.
* **816.2a** While Hyper Mode is released, the creature's Hyper Mode abilities become active in addition to its normal abilities.
* **816.2b** While Hyper Mode is released, the creature's power becomes its Hyper Mode power. During that time, its normal power is not referenced.
* **816.2c** While Hyper Mode is released, the Hyper Mode of that creature cannot be released again.
* **816.2d** While Hyper Mode is released, the Hyperize action (Hyper Mode Release) possessed by that creature cannot be activated.
  * *Example: While the Hyper Mode of 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 is released, the Hyperize action possessed by that 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 cannot be activated.*
**816.3.** The abilities and power of Hyper Mode are not activated while the creature possessing them is in a zone other than the Battle Zone, or while it is in the Battle Zone but Hyper Mode is not released. During that time, it is treated as not possessing the Hyper Mode abilities and power, and they are not referenced.

**817. Dream Rares**
**817.1.** Cards with the Dream Rare rarity possess the special type "Dream."
**817.2.** While an element possessing Dream in its special type (Dream Element), such as a Dream Creature, is in the Battle Zone, its owner cannot put a Dream Element with the same name into the Battle Zone.
  * *Example: While your 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 is in the Battle Zone, you cannot summon or put out another 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》.*
**817.3.** By some method, if a single player ends up having 2 or more Dream Elements with the same name in the Battle Zone, they both remain in the Battle Zone.
  * *Example: Even if 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 with a seal attached is in the Battle Zone, you can put out another 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》. If the attached seal is later removed, 2 copies of 《Dogiragon Ultra, Azure King's Path》 will remain in the Battle Zone.*

**818. Hyper Soul X (Chokon X)**
**818.1.** Some cards possess Hyper Soul X. A card with Hyper Soul X possesses the abilities of Hyper Soul X in addition to its normal abilities.
  * *Example: The Blocker on 《Gallard Sky-A3》 is an ability of Hyper Soul X.*
* **818.1a** The ability of Hyper Soul X is always valid as an ability of that card, exactly the same as normal abilities.
  * *Example: You can put out 《Gallard Sky-A3》 from your hand using the effect of 《Heaven's Gate》. Also, 《Gallard Sky-A3》 existing as a creature in the Battle Zone possesses Blocker.*
* **818.1b** While a card with Hyper Soul X is underneath a creature, that creature gains the abilities of Hyper Soul X. This is an exception to 200.3a.
* **818.1c** Some cards have Soul Icons written on their Hyper Soul X. While these cards are underneath a creature, the creature gains that Soul Icon in addition to the abilities of Hyper Soul X.
  * *Example: 《Evil Heart Taru, Calamity Soul of the Demon God》 has Tamashii (Soul) Soul in its Hyper Soul X. When this is underneath a creature, that creature possesses Tamashii Soul.*

**819. WD Fields**
**819.1.** A WD Field is a type of Twinpact card that contains 2 Fields within 1 card. For the 2 Fields, the correct upright position is the position where their respective names are at the top.
* **819.1a** When executing a WD Field, it enters the Battle Zone in the upright position of the executed Field. When put into the Battle Zone via an effect, it enters the Battle Zone in the upright position of the Field being put out.
  * *Example: If you execute 《Momotenshukaku, Way of Freedom》 by paying a cost of 3, you put it into the Battle Zone in a position where 《Momotenshukaku, Way of Freedom》 is at the top and 《Ryuobujin, Way of the Heart》 is at the bottom.*
  * *Example: When putting out a WD Field from your Mana Zone with the ability of your 《Fiona Forest, Forest Dream Dragon》, you specify one of the sides, and put it into the Battle Zone in a position where that side is at the top.*
* **819.1b** While a WD Field exists in the Battle Zone as a WD Field, only the information of the side that is in the upright position is referenced. However, due to the WD Field's own effects, etc., it may change into the other side's WD Field. When this happens, flip the WD Field upside down so that the other side becomes the upright position.
  * *Example: When your Dragon is destroyed in a situation where 《Momotenshukaku, Way of Freedom》 is in the Battle Zone, 《Momotenshukaku, Way of Freedom》 becomes 《Ryuobujin, Way of the Heart》 by its own ability. At this time, flip 《Momotenshukaku, Way of Freedom》 upside down so that the 《Ryuobujin, Way of the Heart》 side becomes the upright position.*
* **819.1c** When leaving a WD Field in the Battle Zone due to Reconstruction, regardless of the orientation the WD Field was placed in, it remains in the Battle Zone with the side the owner desires as the upright position.

**820. Duel Mates**

**822. G-Castles**
**822.1.** A G-Castle is a special Castle that behaves differently from normal Castles. When referencing card types, it is also treated as a Castle.
**822.2.** The turn player can execute a G-Castle from their hand during their Main Step.
* **822.2a** When a G-Castle is executed, it is turned into a new face-up shield in your Shield Zone.
* **822.2b** Unlike normal Castles, a G-Castle cannot fortify another shield.
**822.3.** When a G-Castle placed face-up in the Shield Zone leaves, it is placed in the Graveyard instead.
